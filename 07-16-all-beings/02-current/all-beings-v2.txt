LYRICS

[Intro: hushed close-mic, single fingerpicked guitar, sparse room air]
Pointed ears. Dirty coat.
A limp that turns into a dance.
He sits, he rolls — ruins the clean I just wiped on.
[two-bar instrumental breath]

[Verse 1: conversational low melody, guitar only, walking pace]
I touch the middle of his skull — both ears fold down.
He stares a beat, then runs like the ground is news.
Fox-face, soft under the grime, twitching mid-step —
every next patch of dust still chosen by him.
Not mine. Not anyone's. Still he finds me.

[Pre-Chorus: near-spoken into melody, soft bass enters]
Not only a new plot. Not only a new friend.
A new way of looking at the street.

[Chorus: full but grounded, controlled — first line unhurried]
Some pets are only things —
never a friend, never almost family.
A rope draws a circle, and that is the world.
Black garage — dust smell before you see him —
a hole chewed under the door
where the longing learned to dig.
Some pets are only things.
Outside is loud. Inside is none of it.
[two-bar instrumental breath, strip toward guitar]

[Verse 2: denser, still controlled, light pad under]
Door locked: eyes go numb — worse than fear.
Door open: he bolts like hunger learned to run.
What did they buy him for — something to manage, something to walk?
"Bring him home" was supposed to mean a duty.
I slide bread through the dark and leave.
I think the free one has more of a life.

[Chorus]

[Bridge: near-spoken parade, one guitar + room; white space between mouths]
"Don't raise that kind of thing."
"Look. Don't touch."
"That dead-lame dog — walking like that —"
[one beat rest]
I stare one second too long.
I decide not to get hurt.
And the dog? He can't hear the words.
[silence half-bar]
"Is he yours?" — fake smile.
I say no. I walk.
Then she curses a body that can't talk back.
"You fear he harms you — he will."
[one beat rest]
"I'll take care of him," I say —
said it before I knew I'd said it.

[Instrumental Break: soft guitar motif, dry light drums, four bars max]

[Verse 3 / 相处: intimate, then open]
With him it's simple —
no coldness to decode, no interest in the kindness,
no fear I paid and won't be paid back.
I treat him straight. He burns the same heat back.
Kids pour around him like he owns the block.
From the angle of closeness,
some people live thinner than the dog.

[Final Chorus: denser layers, late strings only here; controlled peak]
Some pets are only things —
a rope draws a circle, and that is the world.
Under the bridge he stands on two legs, looking for me —
then hits the ground and runs.
Girl on the ramp opens both arms.
He jumps the whole slope like the air owes him.
Some pets are only things.
The street keeps showing faces.
Still not mine. Still free.
[one beat rest]

[Outro: instruments peel; voice alone; air between fragments]
I crouch a little.
I watch.
I want to paint that frame —
and leave the rest unsolved.
[End]
