## 2. Full Lyrics + Vocal Tags

```
[Intro: whisper close-mic, dry fingerpicked guitar, sparse room air — matter-of-fact, no vibrato]
Pointed ears. Dirty coat.
A limp that turns into a dance.
[two-bar instrumental breath]

[Verse 1: conversational low melody, guitar only, walking pace — chest register, dry, not performed]
I touch the middle of his skull — both ears fold down.
Fox-face under grime, twitching mid-step.
Every patch of dust still chosen by him.
Not mine. Not anyone's. Still he finds me.

[Pre-Chorus: near-spoken into melody, soft bass enters — thesis register, flat affect]
Some pets are only things —
never a friend, never almost family.

[Chorus: warm lift, fuller body, declarative — chest voice, direct, no soar]
However long your rope is, I look — don't pull.
We're all held by some rope's radius.
A rope draws a circle — and that is the world.
I don't rescue it. I learn to look.
[two-bar instrumental breath, strip toward guitar]

[Verse 2: denser, matter-of-fact, light brushed snare — dry delivery, gray concrete tone]
Black garage — dust smell before you see him.
Rope on a clip-ring, pale circle worn in the dust.
Door locked: eyes go numb — worse than fear.
A hole chewed under the door where longing learned to dig.
Door open: he bolts like hunger learned to run.
What did they buy him for — to manage, to walk?
After work. I slide bread through the dark and leave.

[Pre-Chorus: same thesis, slightly fuller]
Some pets are only things —
never a friend, never almost family.

[Chorus: identical four lines — same warm hook, controlled peak, no belt]
However long your rope is, I look — don't pull.
We're all held by some rope's radius.
A rope draws a circle — and that is the world.
I don't rescue it. I learn to look.

[Bridge: near-spoken parade, one guitar + room — brittle, flat, white space between mouths]
"Don't raise that kind of thing."
"Look. Don't touch."
"That dead-lame dog — walking like that —"
[one beat rest]
I stare one second too long.
I decide not to get hurt.
And the dog? He can't hear the words.
[silence half-bar]
"You fear he harms you — he will."
Your mortgage. Your shift. Screen glow walking home.
Ropes, all of them. Ropes.

[Verse 3 / Final: intimate open, light lift — warm but unsentimental, no honey]
Under the bridge he stands on two legs, looking for me —
then hits the ground and runs.
Girl on the ramp opens both arms.
He jumps the whole slope like the air owes him.

[Final Chorus: identical first three lines; last line micro-varied only — harmonium swell here, one bar only]
However long your rope is, I look — don't pull.
We're all held by some rope's radius.
A rope draws a circle — and that is the world.
I don't rescue it. I learn to look — and go.
[one beat rest]

[Outro: instruments peel; voice alone; air between fragments — whisper-fact, no fade-to-ethereal]
I crouch a little.
I watch.
I want to paint that frame —
and leave the rest unsolved.
[End]
```
