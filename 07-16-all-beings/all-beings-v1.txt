[Intro: hushed close-mic, single fingerpicked guitar, sparse room air; end on two-bar instrumental breath]
Pointed ears. Dirty coat. A limp that turns into a dance.
He sits, then rolls, then ruins the clean I just wiped on.
[two-bar instrumental breath]

[Verse 1: conversational low melody, guitar only, walking pace, leave air between image lines]
I touch the middle of his skull — both ears fold down.
He stares a beat, then runs like the ground is news.
Fox-face, soft under the grime, a body that twitches mid-step —
free to choose the next patch of dust.
Not mine. Not anyone's. Still he keeps finding me.

[Pre-Chorus: breath closer, soft bass enters, near-spoken into melodic, controlled]
Not only a new plot. Not only a new friend.
A new way of looking at the street.

[Chorus: full but grounded, controlled melodic male — no belting; first line unhurried near-spoken]
Some pets are only things —
a rope draws a circle, and that is the world.
Black garage, dust in the light, a hole chewed under the door
where the longing learned to dig.
Some pets are only things.
Outside is loud. Inside is none of it.
[two-bar instrumental breath, strip toward guitar]

[Verse 2: denser images, still controlled, light pad under guitar]
Door locked: eyes go numb, the kind that scares you more than fear.
Door open: he bolts like hunger learned to run.
What did they buy him for — something to manage, something to walk?
I feed bread through the dark and leave.
I think the free one has more of a life.

[Pre-Chorus: same shape]
Not only a new plot. Not only a new friend.
A new way of looking at the street.

[Chorus: full but grounded, controlled melodic male — no belting]
Some pets are only things —
a rope draws a circle, and that is the world.
Black garage, dust in the light, a hole chewed under the door
where the longing learned to dig.
Some pets are only things.
Outside is loud. Inside is none of it.

[Bridge: near-spoken, one guitar + room tone, wide white space]
"Don't raise that kind of thing."
"Look, don't touch."
"That lame dog — walk like that —"
[one beat rest]
I stare one second too long, then let it go.
I decide not to get hurt.
[silence half-bar — let it hang]
And the dog? He can't hear the words.
[one beat rest]
"I'll take care of him," I say —
said it before I knew I'd said it.

[Instrumental Break: soft guitar motif, dry light drums, four bars max — mid-song exhale, not a solo]

[Final Chorus: denser layers, late strings only here, peak intensity controlled — sung never shouted; one-bar lift-in]
Some pets are only things —
a rope draws a circle, and that is the world.
But under the bridge he stands on two legs, looking for me —
then hits the ground and runs.
Girl on the ramp opens both arms.
He jumps the whole distance home.
Some pets are only things.
Not this one. Not tonight.
[one beat rest]
Still not mine. Still free.
Still the street keeps showing faces.

[Outro: hushed, instruments peel off, voice alone at end, air between fragments]
I crouch a little.
I watch.
I want to paint that frame —
and leave the rest unsolved.
[End]
