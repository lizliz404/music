# EXACTLY ON TIME
### (the idea, speaking) — v2, integrated

## STYLE / GENRE PRODUCTION PROMPT

Reflective alt-pop with indie electronic texture, 80 BPM, F major with recurring lifts into its relative D minor — warm and wonder-filled, deliberately unresolved rather than fully resolved, mirroring the song's open questions. Vocal: female, intimate, breathy, close-mic'd — not performing a diary entry as its writer, but voicing the idea itself: a thought that has waited years to be spoken, slightly otherworldly, a soft knowing smile under the breath, patient rather than nostalgic. Never belted, never theatrical. Journey: soft electric piano, faint vinyl crackle, and tape hiss open the song like something surfacing from sleep. Verse 1 stays sparse — minimal beat, dusty warmth, a single repeating piano figure, like handwriting appearing on a page. Pre-chorus lifts gently with a swelling pad and a second harmony, slightly detached, as if overheard. Chorus opens wider — soft four-on-the-floor kick, warm bass, layered backing vocals that feel like an echo answering itself, not one voice but recognition. Verse 2 adds a subtle pulsing arpeggiated synth — the sound of a thought growing new branches on its own. Bridge strips to just voice and piano, most intimate moment, swells once, then falls back. Final chorus returns fuller and more assured — not louder for its own sake, but settled, like something that has finally arrived where it was always going. Outro fades to tape hiss and the soft click of a notebook closing. Production: warm, analog-leaning, intentional space between notes — nostalgia without sentimentality, wonder without naivety, recursion without resolution.

Exclude styles: dramatic orchestral, trap, metal, belted pop, EDM drop, overproduced, glossy, gospel choir, arena pop, hyperpop, darkwave.

I am the idea he drew —
a hundred days before the exam,
two circles in a margin,
folded shut, and left like that.

A hundred days until the test
and I arrived — early for him, on time for me

He wrote me in the margin of a notebook:
players managing universes,
choosing gravity from a dropdown,
seeding scientists like wildflowers,
the last one standing wins
