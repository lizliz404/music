## 2. Style / Genre Production Prompt (Suno Style Field)

Intimate alt-pop / indie electronic singer-songwriter ballad, contemplative and nostalgic without sadness. Begins as a near-whisper over sparse warm electric piano and faint vinyl crackle, almost like someone reading an old diary aloud. Gradually layers soft analog synth pads, subtle fingerpicked guitar, and muted heartbeat kick. Builds through the chorus with airy stacked harmonies and a gentle pulsing electronic undercurrent — wonder, not drama. Bridge strips nearly bare to breathy falsetto over a single held pad, then swells once with restrained harmonic lift. Final chorus returns fuller but still intimate; outro fades to whispered vocal and piano alone. Female vocal persona: intimate, breathy alto-leaning, close-mic'd, confessional, slight smile in the tone — curious and self-aware, never belted, never theatrical. Mid-tempo, approx 78 BPM, key of F major or D minor (warm, unresolved edges). Soft sidechain, wide but quiet stereo image, lo-fi warmth without muddiness. Exclude: belting, arena pop, EDM drops, heavy drums, theatrical Broadway, trap hi-hats, aggressive rock, gospel choir, darkwave, hyperpop.

## 3. Angle Note

**Emotional core:** The shock of realizing your teenage self was onto something real — and the recursive chill of developing a story about creators while becoming one.

**Structural choice:** Classic ABABCB (Verse / Pre / Chorus / Verse / Pre / Chorus / Bridge / Final Chorus / Outro), but with deliberately flat dynamics — the "climax" is recognition, not volume. The pre-chorus flips meaning between passes ("filed under later" → "it still had a name"). The final chorus rewrites the hook from question to claim: "not nonsense — just weight."

**What makes this version mine:** The title image — two circles, one inside another — is both the literal notebook doodle from class and the meta-structure of the song (girl inside diary inside adult inside song inside listener). The hook "Who's the player, who's the game?" carries the philosophical question without naming simulation theory. Self-deprecation stays grounded ("funny what we say," "Fact is, I still do") rather than precious. AI appears only as "a mind that never sat that class" — process, not product. No plot spoilers; only the feeling of finding them.
