# EXACTLY ON TIME
### (the idea, speaking) — v3

## STYLE / GENRE PRODUCTION PROMPT

Reflective alt-pop with indie electronic texture, 80 BPM, F major with recurring lifts into its relative D minor — warm and wonder-filled, deliberately unresolved rather than fully resolved, mirroring the song's open questions. Target length ~3:30. Vocal: female, intimate, breathy, close-mic'd — not performing a diary entry as its writer, but voicing the idea itself: a thought that has waited years to be spoken, slightly otherworldly, a soft knowing smile under the breath, patient rather than nostalgic. Never belted, never theatrical, never rushed.

Journey: soft electric piano, faint vinyl crackle, and tape hiss open the song like something surfacing from sleep. Verse 1 stays sparse — minimal beat, dusty warmth, a single repeating piano figure, like handwriting appearing on a page. Pre-chorus lifts gently with a swelling pad and a second harmony, slightly detached, as if overheard. Chorus opens wider — muted four-on-the-floor kick (felt, not punched — this tempo asks for weight, not impact), warm bass, layered backing vocals that feel like an echo answering itself, not one voice but recognition. Verse 2 adds a subtle pulsing arpeggiated synth — the sound of a thought growing new branches on its own. Bridge strips to just voice and piano, most intimate moment, swells once on the closing question, then falls back to near-silence before the final chorus.

Final chorus: the climax is harmonic and textural, not dynamic — backing harmonies thicken into fuller close voicings, the piano resolves into richer chords, the mix widens rather than gets louder. Arrives settled and hushed-certain, not big or triumphant.

Outro fades to tape hiss and the soft click of a notebook closing.

Production: warm, analog-leaning, intentional space between notes — nostalgia without sentimentality, wonder without naivety, recursion without resolution.

Exclude styles: dramatic orchestral, trap, metal, belted pop, EDM drop, overproduced, glossy, gospel choir, arena pop, hyperpop, darkwave. Exclude at final chorus specifically: loudness-as-payoff, key-change modulation, drum-fill buildup, gospel-style vocal runs.

## LYRICS

[Intro — tape hiss, soft electric piano, a page turning]
[Female Vocals] [Whispered] [Intimate] [Low Energy]

Chalk dust, floating in the sun.
I am the idea he drew —
a hundred days before the exam,
two circles in a margin,
folded shut, and left like that.

[Verse 1]
[Female Vocals] [Breathy] [Smooth] [Low Energy]

The teacher, distant — a siren in another town
He wasn't taking notes — he was taking me down
Not the kind of thing you study for
the kind that studies you
A hundred days until the test
and I arrived — early for him, on time for me

He wrote me in the margin of a notebook:
players managing universes,
choosing gravity from a dropdown,
seeding scientists like wildflowers
Not a god — a mayor, he wrote
Zoning laws for stars, term limits on the sun

[Pre-Chorus]
[Female Vocals] [Smooth] [Building Energy] [Harmonies]

Ideas don't care about timing —
they show up when they show up.
Some stay folded in a notebook.
Some drag you through a hundred days.
Some just wait for the chalk to settle.

[Chorus]
[Female Vocals] [Intimate → Open] [Medium Energy] [Harmonies]

I'm a circle inside a circle inside a circle
He found me years later — the ink gone soft, the shape still held
The boy who drew me wasn't wrong
He was just early
He was just early

[Verse 2]
[Female Vocals] [Breathy] [Smooth] [Medium Energy]

Now he feeds me to a machine — asks it to grow with me
They rename me — call me experiment,
call me engine, call me anything
but what I am
They don't fully understand what I'm reaching for
and neither does he

I'm growing in directions he never mapped at seventeen —
a player winning by doing almost nothing
a scientist who feels his thoughts aren't entirely his own
and somewhere in my own code, the creeping sense
that whoever's writing me
might be written too

[Pre-Chorus]
[Female Vocals] [Smooth] [Building Energy] [Harmonies]

Ideas don't ask permission —
they take root while you sleep
I faded in a drawer once
came back sharper
than when he left me

[Chorus]
[Female Vocals] [Intimate → Open] [Medium Energy] [Harmonies]

I'm a circle inside a circle inside a circle
He found me years later — the ink gone soft, the shape still held
The boy who drew me wasn't wrong
He was just early
He was just early

[Bridge]
[Female Vocals] [Breathy] [Falsetto] [Intimate] [Low Energy — piano only]

Here's the part nobody tells you about old ideas:
they don't belong to the person you are —
they belong to the person you were,
and meeting them again
is like meeting a stranger
who knows your middle name

What if the game we're playing
is the game that's playing us?

[Final Chorus]
[Female Vocals] [Open] [Medium Energy] [Harmonies] [Hushed Triumph] [Settled, Not Loud]

I'm a circle inside a circle inside a circle —
still waiting, still turning, the ink gone soft, the shape still holds
The boy who drew me wasn't wrong —
he wasn't early
he was exactly on time
and neither, I think, am I

[Outro — beat fades, just piano and tape hiss]
[Female Vocals] [Whispered] [Falling tension]

Chalk dust. Sunlight. Two circles.
A notebook, closing.
I don't need to be right.
I just needed to be found.
[End]
