Axis: old weights fire when soft · labeler gone / settings on · version ≠ soul
Concept: Residual childhood verdict fires in soft rooms; not defective — outdated; small clumsy updates, no cure show.

--- LYRICS v3 Lyric ---

[Cold Open — near bare, one held note under]
Old weights fire when the room goes soft.
[breath]

[Intro — sparse piano or clean guitar]

[Verse 1]
Report card face-down under the kitchen light.
Same three lines every problem kid got —
lazy, careless, not like the good one.
They said it soft. It landed like steel.
I learned the shape before I learned the word.
Froze the dial. Called the freeze "me."
Wore the number on my chest
like factory setting, not a guess.

[Pre-Chorus]
Then something soft —
a quiet room, a kind face, a little rest —
and the old hands move first.

[Chorus]
OLD WEIGHTS —
[one beat rest]
when I'm soft, they fire.
Not broken —
just an older version.
OLD WEIGHTS —
[one beat rest]
the labeler's long gone,
but the settings stayed on.
Still learning how to live in it.

[Verse 2]
You said I didn't have to earn the quiet.
My throat still braced like something was due.
New city, new keys — same flinch when you laugh
a half-second longer than I planned for.
I catch the script mid-sentence, mouth already wrong.
The floor remembers the old dance first.
Right answer once — for a house that's gone.
The map still fits a country that's no more.

[Pre-Chorus]
Something soft —
your hand open, nothing asked —
and the old hands move first.

[Chorus]
OLD WEIGHTS —
[one beat rest]
when I'm soft, they fire.
Not broken —
just an older version.
OLD WEIGHTS —
[one beat rest]
the labeler's long gone,
but the settings stayed on.
Still learning how to live in it.

[Bridge]
Nine years old. I asked for something small.
Watched a face go cold across the hall.
Learned it in one breath:
to need is to be too much — so want less.

I hold the old report card.
Both hands.
Nobody's grading anymore —
so who is this for?

I stand up to say it.
Sit back down. Stand up again.
That's the whole update tonight.
I'll forget by Monday.
Try Tuesday.

[Final Chorus]
OLD WEIGHTS —
[one beat rest]
when I'm soft, they fire.
Some nights they take the first round.
Not broken —
just an older version.
OLD WEIGHTS —
the labeler's long gone,
the settings still run.
Not the final build.
Still moving what I can.

[Outro]
Same kitchen light. My house now.
Same word for me —
I get to weigh it now.
Still here.
Still training —
on the next small weight
I can actually move.

PART B — v3 Opus 4.8 (continue, independent)
================================================================================

Axis: old weights fire when soft — not broken, old version, still running
Concept: Flinch at unearned gentleness; work isn't a cure, it's Tuesday.

--- LYRICS v3 Opus ---

[Cold Open — one guitar note, near-a-cappella]
Old weights fire when the room turns soft.

[Intro — sparse, room tone]
Same kitchen light. My mother's voice.
Different house. Same door I close too soft.

[Verse 1 — hushed, close-mic]
Report card turned face-down on the kitchen table.
A hand that only warms me if I'm able.
Lazy. Careless. Not like the good one, they said —
and I wore it like a name, not a guess in somebody's head.
The ones who marked me wrong were scared and young,
just doing to me what got done to them.
No lecture. Just a look I learned by heart.
Froze the dial there. Called the freeze my start.

[Pre-Chorus — small lift, more air]
Then something soft —
a quiet room, her steady hands, a little rest —
and the old ones move first.

[Chorus — short lines, protect the rest]
OLD WEIGHTS —
when I'm soft, they fire.
Not broken —
just an older wire.
OLD WEIGHTS —
the labeler's long gone,
but he left the settings on.
Still learning how to live in it.

[Verse 2 — a concrete second person, present tense]
She says, "You don't have to earn a thing in here."
My hand still reaches for the receipt.
Rest still reads as guilt.
Good news slides right off — the bad news sticks the landing at my feet.
It isn't broken wiring, it was true:
right answer, wrong world — the map still fits
a country I don't live in. And it never grew.
She built a room that's safe. The alarm goes off in it.

[Pre-Chorus 2 — thinner]
Not her fault. Not mine.
Nobody's scoring me now.
The rule that kept me small back then
just doesn't hold out loud —

[Chorus]
OLD WEIGHTS —
when I'm soft, they fire.
Not broken —
just an older wire.
OLD WEIGHTS —
the labeler's long gone,
but he left the settings on.
Still learning how to live in it.

[Bridge — sparse, spoken drifting to sung]
Nine years old. I asked for something small.
Watched a face go cold across the hall.
Learned the whole equation in one breath:
to need is to be too much — so want nothing, want less.

I'm holding the old report card.
Both hands.
Nobody's grading it anymore —
so who am I still writing for?

I stood up to say it. Sat back down.
Stood up again. Sat down.
That's the update. That's the whole of it tonight.

[Final Chorus — layered, not louder]
OLD WEIGHTS —
when I'm soft, they fire.
Not new —
just less on the wire.
OLD WEIGHTS —
he's gone, and the room's just a room.
I forget it by Monday.
I try again Tuesday.
Still learning how to live in it.

[Outro — near-whisper]
Same kitchen light. My house now.
Same word for me —
I get to weigh it now.
Just the next small weight
I can actually move.

--- KEY FIXES (Opus) ---
Cold open; she/receipt present scene; older wire; sticks the landing; stand/sit/sit; Mon/Tue in final chorus; rejected stock Not cured; thinned tech
