**1. Working title (primary):** *Old Weights* — (alts: *Soft Rooms*, *The Labeler Left the Room*)

**2. One-sentence concept:** A grown kid discovers the cruel judgments about them were never truth — just cheap labels pasted on by people who left, still firing in the quiet moments, and the only work left is not a cure but staying in the loop.

**3. Metaphor axis:** old weights fire when it's soft — not defective, just an outdated version still training.

---

**4. COMPLETE LYRICS**

[Intro — hushed, close-mic, sparse]
Same kitchen light. Different house.
Same word for me, said low, said proud.
Somebody wrote it down in me
before I learned to read.

[Verse 1 — spoken-close, dense]
Report card face-down on the table,
a hand that only warms if I'm able.
"Lazy." "Careless." "That's just how you are."
I wore the sticker like it came from God —
but the ones who labeled me were kids themselves,
scared people copying scared people's help.
No lecture. Just a look I learned by heart —
I became the mirror, not the art.

[Pre-Chorus — lift, more air]
And I ran that code so long
I forgot it wasn't mine.
Set to factory in a room
that closed a long, long time —

[Chorus — melodic, restrained, denser texture]
Old weights fire when the moment goes soft.
Somebody's fear I never asked to be taught.
Not a broken soul — an outdated line,
still running default in the back of my mind.
The labeler left the room. Left the settings on.
I keep re-reading a verdict
from a court that's gone.

[Verse 2 — controlled, a little tighter]
So it works — the flinch when someone's kind,
I brace for the bill behind the smile every time.
Rest reads as guilt. A win reads as a fluke.
The bad news lands, the good just slides off smooth.
That wasn't broken wiring — that was true
in a house where love came priced, and overdue.
Right answer, wrong world. Same as before:
the map still fits a country that's no more.

[Pre-Chorus 2 — thinner]
But the room I train in changed.
Nobody's scoring me now.
The rule that kept me small in there
just doesn't hold out loud —

[Chorus — repeat, slightly opened]
Old weights fire when the moment goes soft.
Somebody's fear I never asked to be taught.
Not a broken soul — an outdated line,
still running default in the back of my mind.
The labeler left the room. Left the settings on.
I keep re-reading a verdict
from a court that's gone.

[Bridge — three moves: wound / question / physical act]
(wound)
Nine years old. I asked for something small.
Watched a face go cold across the hall.
Learned the whole equation in one breath:
to need is to be too much — so want nothing, want less.

(question)
So who gets the pen now? Who says what I am —
the frightened room, or the one still standing?

(physical act)
I put the old report card down.
I say it flat, no crown, no cure:
"That was the version. Not the core."
And I leave the light on. And I close the drawer.

[Final Chorus — same melody, humbled, still training]
Old weights still fire when the moment goes soft —
some nights I lose to a fear I forgot.
Not cured. Not pure. Not rebuilt overnight —
just one more small update, one honest trial.
The labeler's long gone. The room's just a room.
I'm not the sentence they left me.
I'm still in the loop.

[Outro — near-whisper, one breath]
Same kitchen light. My house now.
Same word for me —
I get to weigh it now.
Still training.
Still here.
