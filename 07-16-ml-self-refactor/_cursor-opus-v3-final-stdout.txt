# Old Weights

**Concept:** A kid labeled lazy/careless froze that verdict into identity; the adult's old weights still fire in soft rooms — not broken, just an outdated version still running after the labeler left, still moving small weights instead of staging a cure.

---

[Cold Open]
Old weights fire when the room goes soft.

[Intro]

[Verse 1]
Report card face-down under the kitchen light.
Same three lines every problem kid got —
lazy, careless, not like the good one.
They said it soft. It landed like steel.
I learned the shape before I learned the word.
Froze the dial. Called the freeze "me."
Wore the number on my chest
like factory setting, not a guess.

[Pre-Chorus]
Then something soft —
a quiet room, a kind face, a little rest —
and the old hands move first.

[Chorus]
OLD WEIGHTS —
when I'm soft, they fire.
Not broken —
just an older version.
OLD WEIGHTS —
the labeler's long gone,
but the settings stayed on.
Still learning how to live in it.

[Verse 2]
You said I didn't have to earn the quiet.
My throat still braced like something was due.
New city, new keys — same flinch when you laugh
a half-second longer than I planned for.
I catch the script mid-sentence, mouth already wrong.
The floor remembers the old dance first.
Right answer once — for a house that's gone.
The map still fits a country that's no more.

[Pre-Chorus]
Something soft —
your hand open, nothing asked —
and the old hands move first.

[Chorus]
OLD WEIGHTS —
when I'm soft, they fire.
Not broken —
just an older version.
OLD WEIGHTS —
the labeler's long gone,
but the settings stayed on.
Still learning how to live in it.

[Bridge]
Nine years old. I asked for something small.
Watched a face go cold across the hall.
Learned it in one breath:
to need is to be too much — so want less.

I hold the old report card.
Both hands.
Nobody's grading anymore —
so who is this for?

I stand up to say it.
Sit back down. Stand up again.
That's the whole update tonight.

[Final Chorus]
OLD WEIGHTS —
when I'm soft, they fire.
Some nights they take the first round.
Not broken —
just an older version.
OLD WEIGHTS —
the labeler's long gone,
the settings still run.
I forget it by Monday.
Try again Tuesday.
Not the final build.
Still moving what I can.

[Outro]
Same kitchen light. My house now.
Same word for me —
I get to weigh it now.
Still here.
Still training —
on the next small weight
I can actually move.
