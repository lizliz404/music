I've absorbed both drafts and all three audits. Here's my v3.

---

**1. Working title:** Old Weights

**2. One-sentence concept:** A grown man keeps flinching at gentleness he no longer has to earn — proof he's not broken, just running an old version of himself, and the work isn't a cure, it's Tuesday.

**3. Metaphor axis:** old weights fire when it's soft — not broken, just an old version, still running

---

**4. COMPLETE LYRICS**

[Cold Open — one guitar note, near-a-cappella]
Old weights fire when the room turns soft.

[Intro — sparse, room tone]
Same kitchen light. My mother's voice.
Different house. Same door I close too soft.

[Verse 1 — hushed, close-mic]
Report card turned face-down on the kitchen table.
A hand that only warms me if I'm able.
Lazy. Careless. Not like the good one, they said —
and I wore it like a name, not a guess in somebody's head.
The ones who marked me wrong were scared and young,
just doing to me what got done to them.
No lecture. Just a look I learned by heart.
Froze the dial there. Called the freeze my start.

[Pre-Chorus — small lift, more air]
Then something soft —
a quiet room, her steady hands, a little rest —
and the old ones move first.

[Chorus — short lines, protect the rest]
OLD WEIGHTS —
when I'm soft, they fire.
Not broken —
just an older wire.
OLD WEIGHTS —
the labeler's long gone,
but he left the settings on.
Still learning how to live in it.

[Verse 2 — a concrete second person, present tense]
She says, "You don't have to earn a thing in here."
My hand still reaches for the receipt.
Rest still reads as guilt.
Good news slides right off — the bad news sticks the landing at my feet.
It isn't broken wiring, it was true:
right answer, wrong world — the map still fits
a country I don't live in. And it never grew.
She built a room that's safe. The alarm goes off in it.

[Pre-Chorus 2 — thinner]
Not her fault. Not mine.
Nobody's scoring me now.
The rule that kept me small back then
just doesn't hold out loud —

[Chorus]
OLD WEIGHTS —
when I'm soft, they fire.
Not broken —
just an older wire.
OLD WEIGHTS —
the labeler's long gone,
but he left the settings on.
Still learning how to live in it.

[Bridge — sparse, spoken drifting to sung]
Nine years old. I asked for something small.
Watched a face go cold across the hall.
Learned the whole equation in one breath:
to need is to be too much — so want nothing, want less.

I'm holding the old report card.
Both hands.
Nobody's grading it anymore —
so who am I still writing for?

I stood up to say it. Sat back down.
Stood up again. Sat down.
That's the update. That's the whole of it tonight.

[Final Chorus — layered, not louder]
OLD WEIGHTS —
when I'm soft, they fire.
Not new —
just less on the wire.
OLD WEIGHTS —
he's gone, and the room's just a room.
I forget it by Monday.
I try again Tuesday.
Still learning how to live in it.

[Outro — near-whisper]
Same kitchen light. My house now.
Same word for me —
I get to weigh it now.
Just the next small weight
I can actually move.

**5. Changelog**

Taken from **v1:**
- Beat-rest chorus cadence (short lines, "OLD WEIGHTS —" then rest) — audits agreed this is the more hummable, singable hook; borrowed the cadence, not every word.
- "Froze the dial there. Called the freeze my start." — v1's tightest internalization image, compressed further.
- Outro image "the next small weight I can actually move" — audits flagged "actually" as a weak adverb but the payoff (weight→title callback) is the best ending image; kept it, cut v1's redundant "Not the final build" scaffolding around it.

Taken from **v2:**
- Full backbone: kitchen light / map-and-country / "to need is to be too much" / weigh-it-now closing loop.
- "the labeler's long gone, but he left the settings on" — every audit called this the single best line; kept, moved into the compressed chorus so the hook carries the concept.
- Nine-year-old bridge wound kept nearly verbatim (audits: strongest line in either draft).
- Verse 2's "right answer, wrong world / map still fits" distribution-shift image kept, but re-anchored to the present-tense scene.

Audit ideas **accepted:**
- Cold-open signature line before the narrative (streaming pre-hook) — cheap, reorders not rewrites, doesn't dilute.
- Lowered tech density: dropped "code / default / training loop / build / version→wire swap" jargon; kept only the anchor `old weights` + `fire when soft`. Body images now do the work.
- Concrete second person in present tense ("She says, you don't have to earn a thing") — turns monologue into a relationship the listener can enter; puts the residual fire in a real safe room.
- Broke the AABB symmetry ("Good news slides off — the bad news sticks the landing") — de-AI'd the too-tidy couplet; "sticks the landing" is embodied.
- Smaller, failed, un-heroic bridge act ("Stood up… sat down… stood up again") + everyday relapse ("forget it by Monday, try again Tuesday") — makes "still training" carry real weight instead of a completed exit.
- Removed sung structural labels — no "(wound)/(question)/(act)" or "[one beat rest]" text a generator might sing.
- Fixed "table light" ambiguity → "kitchen table."

Audit ideas **rejected / softened:**
- Stock "Not cured. Not pure. / some days I still lose" ending — Liz archived it as path-dependency; replaced the residual-honesty with the Monday/Tuesday scene + physical relapse, which shows the same thing without the borrowed slogan.
- Full parameterized style-prompt / per-line delivery map from audit #3 — useful but out of scope for a lyric deliverable; condensed to the short note below.
- "no melisma / Isbell / Sufjan reference-lock" hard baselines — treated as optional, not law per the no-factory-path policy.
- Kept concept density fairly high rather than cutting to the audit's suggested 25% tech — the conceit is the reason the song exists; I thinned jargon but didn't gut it.

**6. Genre / production note (this song only)**
Confessional alt-folk / chamber-indie, ~78–82 BPM, minor or D-dorian color. Fingerpicked nylon or clean neck-pickup guitar opening; felt piano and upright bass entering by the pre-chorus; brushed drums only from the second chorus; one low octave vocal double on the final chorus, no gang vocals. Close-mic, dry, audible breath, conversational-melodic; chest voice, no falsetto, no belting — peak dynamic stays restrained (diagnosis, not lament). Bridge nearly spoken, one live-take feel with room for a small vocal hesitation.

Exclude: screaming, belting, grit, pop-punk, emo, EDM drops, autotune-heavy, anthemic stadium swell, cure-anthem triumph, worship-pad build.
