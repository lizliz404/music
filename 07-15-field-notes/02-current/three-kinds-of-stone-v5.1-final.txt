[Intro: hushed close-mic, single fingerpicked guitar, sparse room air, almost spoken; end on two-bar instrumental breath, do not crowd the last line]
Sixteen floors. Thirty-two doors on this floor alone.
Neighbors close enough to share a wall — far enough to never knock.
Headphones on. I run the feed for the next bright thing.
I put my socks on. I open the door.
[two-bar instrumental breath]

[Verse 1: conversational low melody, fingerpicked guitar only, walking pace, unhurried, leave space between image lines]
Frosted slab. Old bluestone that won't lie flat.
River-round cobble that rolls under the sole.
I slow like a kid and read the street with the feet —
three different weights, three different truths in the bone.
Cicadas swell and thin. A butterfly leaves one white stroke —
lands without a word on the bank.
Cranes bang near and far at the same thin second —
the sound has weight; the phone in my pocket has none.

[Pre-Chorus: breath closer, soft bass enters, near-spoken into melodic, controlled, tightening]
My head can run the whole day clean and never leave the room,
reading the world from somewhere just above the wound.
The glass stays flat under the thumb. The street doesn't.
It asks for skin. It asks for time.

[Chorus: full but grounded, controlled melodic male — no belting, brushed drums, warm bass; first line delivered as unhurried near-spoken descent, syllables even, no rising pop melody]
I was living like a passenger in a body I forgot how to drive —
brain at the wheel of nothing, floating two inches above the ride.
Quiet number eleven — not the fire, not the rush —
even the riders coast; no one is racing the dust.
So I put my weight down.
[one beat rest]
I put my weight down.
Stone under the shoe. Glass in the hand.
I came out of the screen to feel where the bluestone breaks.
[two-bar instrumental breath, strip toward guitar, let the hook settle]

[Verse 2 — stanza A (dogs): conversational return, light pad under guitar, intimate, mid density]
Two dogs at a kitchen door — grey-black, dirty white —
oil-smoke in the fur, already against my legs.
Not pretty. Not soft. They press me anyway.
Something in the skin that symbols never fed
stops asking the feed for a body.

[Verse 2 — stanza B (grandma): drop density, sparser, lower, near-spoken, more air between lines]
Grandma at the stop: which bus, which when?
I open the little program I never need.
A small kindness moves. My clever mouth goes quiet.
Day one, and I still haven't heard a temper break.

[Pre-Chorus: same shape as first pre, bass warmer, controlled, tightening]
My head can run the whole day clean and never leave the bed,
reading the world from somewhere just above the wound.
The glass stays flat under the thumb. The street doesn't.
It asks for skin. It asks for time.

[Chorus: full but grounded, controlled melodic male — no belting, brushed drums, warm bass; first line delivered as unhurried near-spoken descent, syllables even, no rising pop melody]
I was living like a passenger in a body I forgot how to drive —
brain at the wheel of nothing, floating two inches above the ride.
Quiet number eleven — not the fire, not the rush —
even the riders coast; no one is racing the dust.
So I put my weight down.
[one beat rest]
I put my weight down.
Stone under the shoe. Glass in the hand.
I came out of the screen to feel where the cobble rolls.

[Bridge: near-spoken, stripped to one guitar + room tone, wide white space between lines]
Ten square meters. Blinds down. Headphones on.
Chasing the next bright thing till the body felt
gone.
Am I living in the skin —
or is the body just a car
for a brain that only trusts what lights up far?
[silence half-bar — let the question hang, no instruments]
I set the phone face-down on the floor.
Then I put my socks on. Then I opened the door.

[Instrumental Break: soft wood-court motif, dry light drums, four bars max, melodic not technical — this is the mid-song exhale, not a solo]

[Final Chorus: denser layers, late strings only here, peak intensity controlled — sung never shouted; one-bar lift-in, do not wall-to-wall; first line unhurried near-spoken descent]
I was living like a passenger in a body I forgot how to drive —
brain at the wheel of nothing, floating two inches above the ride.
Quiet number eleven — trouble I dragged here still waits outside.
Still close, still far. Still glass in the pocket, stone under the stride.
So I put my weight down.
[one beat rest]
I put my weight down.
Not cured. Not pure. The main thing stays unsolved.
Socks on. Door ajar. Feet on the stone of the world.

[Outro: hushed, instruments peel off one by one, voice alone at the end, air between fragments]
Frosted slab. Old bluestone. River-round cobble.
One more block with weight.
Not floating.
Not finished.
Just found the ground again —
and the problems still wait.
[End]
