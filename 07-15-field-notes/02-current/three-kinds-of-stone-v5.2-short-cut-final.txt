LYRICS
================================================================================

[Intro: hushed close-mic, single fingerpicked guitar, sparse room air, almost spoken; distant female wordless hum enters only in the final two-bar breath]

Sixteen floors. Thirty-two doors on this floor alone.
Neighbors close enough to share a wall — far enough to never knock.
I put my socks on. I open the door.

[two-bar instrumental breath]

[Verse 1: conversational low melody, fingerpicked guitar only, walking pace, leave space between image lines]

Frosted slab. Old bluestone that won't lie flat.
River-round cobble that rolls under the sole.
I slow like a kid and read the street with the feet —
three different weights, three different truths in the bone.
Cranes bang near and far at the same thin second —
the sound has weight; the phone in my pocket has none.

[Pre-Chorus: breath closer, soft bass enters, near-spoken into melodic, controlled tightening]

My head can run the whole day clean and never leave the room,
reading the world from somewhere just above the wound.
The glass stays flat under the thumb. The street doesn't.
It asks for skin. It asks for time.

[Chorus 1: full but grounded, controlled male vocal; first line an unhurried near-spoken descent, even syllable flow, no rising pop melody]

I was living like a passenger in a body I forgot how to drive —
floating two inches above the ride.
Quiet number eleven — not the fire, not the rush —
So I put my weight down.

[one beat rest]

I put my weight down.
Stone under the shoe. Glass in the hand.
I came out of the screen to feel where the bluestone breaks.

[two-bar instrumental breath, strip toward guitar]

[Verse 2 — dogs: conversational return, intimate, moderate density]

Two dogs at a kitchen door — grey-black, dirty white —
oil-smoke in the fur, already against my legs.
Not pretty. Not soft. They press me anyway.

[Verse 2 — grandma: drop density, lower and sparser, more air]

Grandma at the stop: which bus, which when?
I open the little program I never need.
A small kindness moves. My clever mouth goes quiet.

[Short Refrain: bass warm, drums remain restrained; no full rebuild]

So I put my weight down.

[one beat rest]

I put my weight down.
I came out of the screen to feel where the cobble rolls.

[Bridge: near-spoken, stripped to one guitar and room tone, wide white space]

Ten square meters. Blinds down. Headphones on.
Chasing the next bright thing till the body felt
gone.
Am I living in the skin —
or is the body just a car
for a brain that only trusts what lights up far?

[silence half-bar — no instruments]

I set the phone face-down on the floor.
Then I put my socks on. Then I opened the door.

[Instrumental Turnaround: soft wood-court motif, dry light drums, two bars only; melodic exhale, not a solo]

[Final Chorus: one-bar lift-in, denser but controlled; restrained late strings only here, sung never shouted]

Quiet number eleven — trouble I dragged here still waits outside.
Still close, still far. Still glass in the pocket, stone under the stride.
So I put my weight down.

[one beat rest]

I put my weight down.
Not cured. Not pure. The main thing stays unsolved.
Socks on. Door ajar. Feet on the stone of the world.

[Outro: instruments peel off; male voice close, distant female wordless hum behind the fragments only]

One more block with weight.
Not floating.
Not finished.

[End]
