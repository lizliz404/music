## 1. Title · Concept · Axis

**Title:** *Passenger Body*  
**Alt titles:** *Weight Underfoot* · *Two Planes* · *Soles and Glass*

**One-line concept:**  
A quiet #11-GDP city walk where the body stops being a vehicle for a floating brain — feet relearn three stones while the glass keeps offering symbols instead of weight.

**Axis phrase (ONE metaphor, whole song):**  
**feet / ground / skin / weight**  vs  **glass / screen / symbols / float**  
(= atom world vs bit world)

**Undertow (show, don’t name):**  
Derealization as suspension — life as processing symbols while the body carries the brain like luggage. Not “stayed home too long.” Floating quality via images.

[Intro: hushed close-mic, single clean guitar figure, sparse room air — voice almost spoken-sung]
Sixteen floors. Thirty-two doors on this floor alone.
Everyone near enough to touch. Nobody to hold.
I run the loop: feed, feed, feed the glass —
symbols bright, body parked like a car outside the house.

[Verse 1: conversational low melody, fingerpicked guitar only, walking pace, light density]
Frosted slab. Old blue stone that won't lie flat.
River-round cobble that rolls under the sole.
I slow down like a child and let the street teach the feet —
three different weights, three different truths in the bone.

Cicadas rise and fall. A butterfly won't speak.
It leaves one white line in the air, then holds still on the bank.
Cranes bang far and near at the same thin second —
the sound has weight; the phone in my pocket has none.

[Pre-Chorus: breath closer, soft bass enters, density steps up half a shade]
A screen is one hard plane under the thumb —
always the same cold glass, always the same flat hum.
Out here the ground keeps changing its mind —
it asks for skin. It asks for time.

[Chorus: full but restrained arrangement, controlled sung melody — no belting, hook lands clean]
I was living like a passenger in a body I forgot how to drive —
brain at the wheel of nothing, floating two inches above the ride.
This is the quiet city, number eleven, no fire in the sky —
even the riders coast; no one is racing to die.
So I put my weight down. I put my weight down.
Atom world underfoot. Bit world in the hand.
Not cured. Just heavier than the glass can stand.

[Verse 2: conversational return, acoustic + muted keys, still intimate, mid density]
Two diner dogs, grey-black and dirty white —
oil-smoke in the fur, soft against my legs like night.
Coats aren't pretty. They press me anyway —
and something hungry in the skin gets fed and looks away.

Grandma at the bus stop: which one comes soon?
I open a little app I never use, watch kindness move the room.
You think the frontier is symbols talking back —
someone here can't work the phone. Keep that fact.

[Pre-Chorus: same delivery map as first pre, bass warmer]

[Chorus: identical words, arrangement a shade wider, vocal still controlled and melodic]
I was living like a passenger in a body I forgot how to drive —

even the riders coast; no one is racing to die.

Atom world underfoot. Bit world in the hand.

[Bridge: stripped near-spoken, single guitar, white space between lines — three moves only]
Ten square meters, blinds down, headphones on —
chasing the next bright thing till the body felt gone.
Am I living in the skin, or is the body just a car
for a brain that only trusts what lights up far?
I put the phone face-down on the floor.
Then I put my socks on. Then I opened the door.

[Instrumental Break: soft wood-court motif, dry light drums, four bars, melodic not technical]

[Final Chorus: denser layers, strings optional late only, peak intensity controlled — still sung, never shouted]
I was living like a passenger in a body I forgot how to drive —
brain at the wheel of nothing, floating two inches above the ride.

Still close, still far. Still glass in the pocket, stone under the stride.
So I put my weight down. I put my weight down.
Not pure. Not fixed. The main thing stays unsolved.
Socks on. Door ajar. Feet on the floor of the world.

[Outro: hushed, instruments peel off one by one, voice alone at the end]
Frosted slab. Old blue stone. River-round cobble.
One more block with weight.
Not floating.
Not finished.
Just found the ground again —
and the problems still wait.
