# Field Notes — v3 Lyric (axis-hardened + complete Suno package)

**Title (primary):** Three Kinds of Stone  
**Alts:** Real Floors / Out of the Glass / Quiet Number Eleven  
**Axis:** bit (glass / symbols / float) vs atom (stone / weight / skin)  
**Mode:** ethnography · observation notes · prose-song  
**Length target:** ~3:10  
**Date:** 2026-07-15  

---

## Concept

Not “city tourism.” A body that has been running as the brain’s vehicle  
walks a quiet #11 city and re-learns that floors change.  
The digital is real — symbols, feed, abstraction — but it only moves cognition.  
Long-term only-that: life starts to feel like processing information, not living.  
No cure. Socks on. Door ajar. Main problems still on the table.

## COMPLETE LYRICS (parameterized tags in place)

```
[Intro: hushed close-mic, sparse fingerpicked guitar, almost spoken]
Sixteen floors. Thirty-two doors on this floor.
Neighbors close enough to share a wall — far enough to never knock.
Glass in my hand. Same flat world under the thumb.
I put my socks on. I open the door.

[Verse 1: conversational low male, guitar only, unhurried walking pace]
Frosted slab. Old bluestone. River-round cobble.
I slow like a kid and read the street with the soles.
Cicadas swell and thin. A butterfly leaves one white stroke —
lands without a word on the bank.
Cranes bang near and far at the same time.
Both. At the same time.

[Pre-Chorus: slight denser, soft bass enters, still controlled]
A screen is one hard surface — never changes under the hand.
Symbols, feed, clean light — my head can live there fine.
But my feet don't know a thing about any of that.

[Chorus: full but grounded, controlled melodic male — no belting, brushed drums]
Quiet number eleven —
not the fire, not the rush.
I came out of the glass
to feel what the real floors are.
So close and so far.
Bit in the pocket. Atom under the shoe.
Not to fix me —
just to stand where the living ones do.

[Verse 2: conversational return, light pad under guitar, intimate]
Two dogs at a kitchen door — grey-black, dirty white,
oil-smoke in the fur, already against my legs.
Not pretty. Not soft. They press me anyway.
Something in the skin that symbols never fed
stops asking the feed for a body.

Grandma at the stop: which bus, which when?
I open the little program I never need.
A small kindness moves. My clever mouth goes quiet.
Somewhere a rider coasts — not sprinting the street.
Day one, and I still haven't heard a temper break.

[Pre-Chorus: same shape, controlled]
A screen is one hard surface — never changes under the hand.
I can process a whole day without leaving the bed.
But my feet don't know a thing about any of that.

[Chorus: full but grounded, controlled melodic — no belting]
Quiet number eleven —
not the fire, not the rush.
I came out of the glass
to feel what the real floors are.
So close and so far.
Bit in the pocket. Atom under the shoe.
Not to fix me —
just to stand where the living ones do.

[Bridge: near-spoken, stripped to one guitar + room tone, white space]
Ten square meters. Blinds down.
Headphones on. Wheel turning. Next brightest thing.
Living people only as light on glass —
I could feel myself thinning to a screen.

Is the body home —
or just the car the mind is driving?

Phone face-down on the floor.
Soft wood court under the shoes —
sixteen-nine in, ball comes back all wrong,
my own dribble feels like a stranger's in the bone.
Kids between games flip portrait, landscape, portrait —
don't call them lost. That's a snapshot. Not the whole.

[Final Chorus: denser arrangement, still controlled vocal — conviction without volume]
Quiet number eleven —
not the fire, not the cure.
I came out of the glass
to feel what the real floors are.
Still close. Still far.
Trouble I dragged here is still waiting, I'm not gonna lie.
Not cured. Not pure.
Socks on. Door ajar.
Main thing unsolved —
but I'm not only a passenger tonight.

[Outro: hushed, instruments drop one by one, almost spoken]
Frosted slab. Old bluestone. River-round cobble.
One more block. Feet on the ground.
Not fixed.
Just weighted.
Just found.
```
