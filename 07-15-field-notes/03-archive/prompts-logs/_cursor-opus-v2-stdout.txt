**2. One-sentence concept:** A screen-hermit walks out into a quiet #11-GDP city and lets his body re-learn the ground, animals, and strangers one texture at a time — anthropologist and starving animal in the same skin.

**4. COMPLETE LYRICS**

```
[Intro — hushed, close-mic, melodic]
Sixteen floors, thirty-two doors on mine,
neighbors I'll never learn, close and far, close and far.
Headphones in, I run the wheel again —
then I put my socks on. Then I opened the door.

[Verse 1]
Frosted slab, then old bluestone, then a run of river-round cobblestone,
I slow down like a kid and read the street with the soles of my feet.
Cicadas swell and drop, a butterfly won't talk,
it just leaves a white streak in the air and holds still on the bank.
Cranes are pounding far away and near at the very same time —
both, at the very same time.

[Pre-Chorus]
And a screen is one hard surface, always the same under your hand,
but the ground out here keeps changing, and it wants you to understand —

[Chorus]
this one's the quiet city, number eleven, no fire in the sky,
no one's shouting, even the riders aren't in a hurry to fly.
So close and so far, so close and so far —
I came out of the glass to feel what the real floors are.
Not to fix me. Just to stand where the living ones are.

[Verse 2]
Two dirty dogs at a diner, grey-black and dirty-white,
they smell like kitchen oil and they lean on my legs like they've known me all night.
Fur's not soft, coats aren't pretty, they press me anyway —
and something starving in my hands gets fed and looks away.
A grandma at the bus stop asks me which one's coming soon,
I open the little app for her, watch a small kindness move through the room.
You think you're out on the frontier 'cause you can talk to machines —
someone here can't work a phone. Remember what that means.

[Chorus]
This is the quiet city, number eleven, no fire in the sky,
old blocks lean on new bridges and somehow the seam holds tight.
So close and so far, so close and so far —
I came out of the glass to feel what the real floors are.
Not to fix me. Just to stand where the living ones are.

[Bridge — denser, then white space]
I remember the room: ten square meters, blinds down, chasing the next brightest thing,
the wheel would never stop, and I could feel myself thinning to a screen.
(question) So what am I actually afraid of — a hello, a no, the weight of a face?
(act) I set the phone down flat on the floor.

Then I found the soft wood court — softest floor I've ever known,
the ball came back all wrong, my own dribble felt like a stranger's, unsure of the bone.
Sixteen-nine to get in. The kids between games flip their phones, portrait, landscape, portrait —
don't call them lost. That's just the snapshot. That's just now.

[Final Chorus — intense, controlled]
It's the quiet city, number eleven, no fire in the sky,
and the trouble I dragged here is still waiting, I'm not gonna lie.
Still close, still far. Still close, still far.
Not cured, not pure — I just walked back to where the real floors are.
The main thing's unsolved. But my socks are on. And the door is ajar.

[Outro — hushed]
Frosted slab, old bluestone, river-round cobblestone —
one more block, feet on the ground.
Not fixed. Just here. Just found.
```
