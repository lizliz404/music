- **What it is:** a quiet, spoken-sung folk song about a guy who feels like he's floating outside his own body from too much screen time, and walks outside to feel real ground under his feet again.

## B. Decision

**Is a new version necessary? Yes — one, and only one.**

Because Liz's #1 complaint this turn is *understanding cost*, and the current package does not fix it — v5.2b fixed the **production** risk (style stuffing, length, cold open) but left the **listener** risk (private codes, non-repeating hook) untouched. Those are different problems. v5.2b was an engineering patch; v5.3-market is the listener patch. Shipping v5.2b as-is means shipping a song with a skip at its own chorus.

**Why it's genuinely necessary and not perfectionism:** the three blockers are not taste-polish — they are places where a stranger *loses the thread*. Fixing "number eleven" and "the little program" is not making the song "more beautiful"; it's making it *legible*. That's exactly the debt Liz named.

**The trap to avoid:** the audit-echo trap. Five prior audits all said "opaque but keep it," "stop editing," "you love it too much to let it go." That consensus is itself the 闭门造车 — a workshop agreeing to protect a private code because it's charming *to people who know the backstory*. The counter-trap is equally real: do **not** over-correct into self-help mush ("come back to yourself," "feel alive again"). The fix must swap private code for **equally physical, equally strange** public evidence — not for therapy vocabulary. Keep the soul (body evidence, no cure); lower the toll (private numbers, invisible programs).

**Second trap:** don't re-expand. Every gain here comes from *substitution and tighter repetition*, not from adding lines. Word count must stay flat or drop.
