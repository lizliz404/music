## A) Revised lyrics — v3

Primary title: **Three Kinds of Stone** (subtitle: *close and far*)

```
[Intro: hushed close-mic melodic, single fingerpicked guitar, field-recording cicadas under, unhurried]
Sixteen floors, thirty-two doors on mine,
faces I'll never learn — close and far, close and far.
Headphones in, I ran the wheel all day for the next brightest thing,
till my life felt like reading, not living. So I put my socks on.

[Verse 1: hushed close-mic melodic, fingerpicked guitar only, sparse upright bass, unhurried]
Frosted slab, then old bluestone, then a run of river-round cobble,
I slowed down like a kid and read the street with the soles of my feet.
The glass gave me symbols, gave me signal, gave me a world I could hold in one hand,
but the ground kept changing under me — that's a thing you can't understand from a screen.
Cicadas swell and drop; a butterfly won't talk,
just leaves a white streak in the air and holds still on the bank.

[Pre-Chorus: near-spoken to melodic, add brushed percussion low, controlled]
I'd been floating a little too long, half a signal, half a man —
and the weight of a stone said, come back down, come back down where you stand.

[Chorus: melodic controlled, denser but never loud, warm bass + brushes, vocal stays intimate]
This is the quiet city, number eleven, no fire in the sky,
no one's shouting, even the riders here aren't in a hurry to fly.
Am I living in the body, or is the body just a car
driving the brain around — close and far, close and far?
I came out of the glass to feel what the real floors are.

[Verse 2: hushed close-mic melodic, fingerpicked guitar, faint room ambience, unhurried]
Two dirty dogs at a diner, grey-black and dirty-white,
they smell like kitchen oil and lean on my legs like they've known me all night.
Fur isn't soft, coats aren't pretty — they press on me anyway,
and the hunger that lived in my hands gets fed, and it looks away.
A grandma at the bus stop asks me which one's coming soon;
I open the little app for her, and a small warm current moves through the room.
You think you're way out on the frontier 'cause you can speak to machines —
someone here can't work a phone. Remember what that means.

[Chorus: melodic controlled, denser, add cello long-tones underneath, vocal intimate]
This is the quiet city, number eleven, no fire in the sky,
old blocks lean on new bridges and somehow the seam holds tight.
Am I living in the body, or is the body just a car
driving the brain around — close and far, close and far?
I came out of the glass to feel what the real floors are.

[Bridge: near-spoken dense then wide white space, guitar drops to single notes, then silence, intense-controlled]
Ten square meters, blinds pulled down, chasing the next bright screen,
the wheel would never stop, and I could feel myself thinning to a signal, thinning to a screen.
So what am I so scared of — a hello, a no, the weight of one real face?
I set the phone down flat. Face to the floor. And I stood up in the empty space.

[Verse 3 / Turn: melodic controlled, guitar returns fuller, brushes steady, unhurried]
Then the soft wood court — the softest floor I've ever known,
the ball came back all wrong, my own dribble felt like a stranger's in the bone.
Sixteen-nine to get in. Kids between games flip the phone, portrait, landscape, portrait —
don't call them lost. That's just the snapshot. Not the endgame. Just now.

[Final Chorus: intense-controlled, fullest arrangement, late strings swell, vocal still close and unforced]
It's the quiet city, number eleven, no fire in the sky,
and the trouble I dragged here is still standing at the door — I won't lie.
Still living in the body, learning the body's not a car;
it's the house, it's the ground, it's how I know how near things really are.
Not cured, not pure — I just walked back to where the real floors are.

[Outro: hushed close-mic melodic, back to single guitar, cicadas return, fade unhurried]
Frosted slab, old bluestone, river-round cobblestone —
one more block, feet on the ground.
Not fixed. Just here. Just found.
Close and far. Close and far.
```

## B) Style prompts — 3 distinct containers

### Container 1 — "Field-Recording Folk" (the walk itself)

**Style of Music:**
Intimate acoustic indie-folk, fingerpicked nylon guitar, upright bass, brushed drums, field-recording ambience (cicadas, river, distant construction), close-mic near-whisper vocal, warm and dry, mid-tempo walking pace, sparse.

**Exclude Styles:**
pop, mainstream radio polish, autotune, trap hi-hats, EDM drops, choir pad spam, synth-pop, spoken-word only, neon, dream-pop, pop-punk, emo, industrial, grunge, screaming, shouting, belting, lo-fi, garage rock, excessive distortion, long guitar solos, rough production, arena rock, power ballad, loud forward vocal

**Sliders:** Weirdness 15-25% · Style Influence 45-55%

**Why it fits:** Ambience literalizes atom-world (real air, real ground) while the near-whisper keeps the floating undertow — you hear a body moving through space, not a stage.

---

### Container 2 — "Chamber Art-Pop with Strings" (the thinking-off)

**Style of Music:**
Restrained chamber art-pop, muted electric guitar, felt piano, cello and viola long-tones, subtle programmed pulse under live brushes, controlled intimate vocal, dynamics by density not volume, late string swell, contemplative.

**Exclude Styles:**
pop, mainstream radio polish, autotune, trap hi-hats, EDM drops, choir pad spam, synth-pop, spoken-word only, neon, dream-pop, pop-punk, emo, industrial, grunge, screaming, shouting, belting, lo-fi, garage rock, excessive distortion, long guitar solos, rough production, arena rock, power ballad, loud forward vocal

**Sliders:** Weirdness 25-35% · Style Influence 55-65%

**Why it fits:** Strings entering late = the body coming back online; the pulse under live instruments stages bit-vs-atom (machine grid vs breathing players) without a lecture.

---

### Container 3 — "Ambient Slowcore / Post-Folk" (the depersonalized haze)

**Style of Music:**
Ambient slowcore post-folk, spacious reverbless-close vocal over airy processed guitar, drone bed, minimal heartbeat percussion, wide silences, hushed and hypnotic, slow build to a controlled peak, textural.

**Exclude Styles:**
pop, mainstream radio polish, autotune, trap hi-hats, EDM drops, choir pad spam, synth-pop, spoken-word only, neon, dream-pop, pop-punk, emo, industrial, grunge, screaming, shouting, belting, lo-fi, garage rock, excessive distortion, long guitar solos, rough production, arena rock, power ballad, loud forward vocal

**Sliders:** Weirdness 35-50% · Style Influence 40-50%

**Why it fits:** Drone + wide silence sonically renders the "thinning to a signal" floating state; the slow build to a controlled (never loud) peak is the re-entry into the physical, mirroring the lyric arc.

## C) Song profile

- **Title:** Three Kinds of Stone (*close and far*)
- **BPM range:** 78–90 (walking pace; Container 3 can drift to 68–74)
- **Key guess:** D major with a leaning-to-relative-B-minor feel — grounded but unresolved; or capo-2 open-C shapes for the folk container
- **Length:** ~3:10–3:25
- **Hook line:** "Am I living in the body, or is the body just a car / driving the brain around — close and far, close and far?"
- **Anti-boring devices:**
  - **Motif:** "frosted slab, old bluestone, river-round cobble" recurs at Intro / Verse 1 / Outro — a tactile refrain that shrinks each time (full list → single line → fragment).
  - **Refrain mutation:** "close and far" migrates from tower (people) → chorus (body/brain) → outro (bare) so the same words re-mean.
  - **Rhythm tension:** dense near-spoken bit-world lines (symbols/signal) vs wide-breath atom-world lines (stone/wind/face) — verbal density is the arrangement.
  - **Sensory swap:** screen = "one hard surface, always the same under your hand"; ground = "kept changing" — touch, not thesis, carries the axis.
  - **Late strings / drone** reserved for the final chorus only = the body coming back online.
  - **No cure:** "trouble still standing at the door… not cured, not pure" keeps the stake open.
