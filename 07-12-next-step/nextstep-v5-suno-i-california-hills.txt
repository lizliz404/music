# "Next Step" v5 — Suno Ready
# Style I-v5: FULL DIAGNOSIS INTEGRATION — The Four Surgeries Applied
# BPM/Key: 82 BPM, D minor

### Style Prompt
Brooding tense unresolved noir folk, 82 BPM D minor. Weathered low baritone close-mic'd with dry consonants, conversational level — Springsteen Nebraska weight, not quiet not forward. Dry drums: kick pushes half-beat ahead of snare, no crashes no fills — heartbeat racing against walking pace. Low-D Hammond drone sustained throughout, bending to Eb under tension — a half-step wound that never closes, slow Leslie, almost subliminal. Clean electric guitars in delayed call-and-response — Guitar B answers semitone higher with quarter-tone bends that never resolve. In final chorus they finally play unison. Descending unresolved bassline, occasionally holds a note one bar longer than expected. Male unison vocal double on choruses — his own voice layered from a distance. Tense strings rise at finale only. Dynamics build through density and dissonance, not volume. Haunted weight.

### Exclude Styles
pop, pop-punk, emo, radio polish, autotune, trap, EDM, synth-pop, dream-pop, belting, shouting, screaming, lo-fi, garage rock, excessive distortion, guitar shredding, arena rock, hard rock, country rock, cheerful, breezy, campfire, singalong, uplifting, major-key resolution, female lead vocal, female backing vocals, choir, cinematic climax, forward vocal, loud mix, driving drums, epic build, prominent bass, driving aggressive bassline, crash cymbals, heavy drum fills

Weirdness: 25-35 · Style Influence: 78-88
