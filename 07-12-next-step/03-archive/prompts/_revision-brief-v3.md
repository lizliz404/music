## The Audits' Key Findings (READ THE FULL AUDIT FILE)

### What the audits agree on:
- The song is a solid 7.4-8.3/10 — good but not a masterpiece
- The biggest flaw: **the raw recording's "manic"/unfiltered quality was sanitized**. The original 117s recording has 23+ repetitions of "I don't even know" with "hmm/oh/yeah" filler words, sung without caring about passersby. The lyrics turned this into a tidy, "respectable" song.
- "过程即奖励" (the process itself is the reward) — Liz's core philosophy — is absent from the lyrics
- Two clear line-level flubs need fixing
- The song needs LESS polish, MORE rawness

### Three surgical fixes the audit recommends:


**Fix 3: Fix two hard flubs**
- Cursor V1: "the road that brought me shame and flame" → rhyme-driven, not meaning-driven. Replace with something cleaner.
- Hermes V1: "I lay there like I'd already lost and won" → cliché. Replace with something more sensory.

## Critical Craft Rules (from Liz's preferences)

- **ALL ENGLISH** lyrics. No Chinese.
- **NO screaming, shouting, belting**. Controlled, sung, melodic, incisive vocals.
- **Images over verdicts** — show concrete scenes, don't state theses.
- **Unified metaphor axis**: transit / walking / luggage / next step.
- **Bridge: exactly 3 moves** — one wound, one question, one physical act.
- **Ending**: NO cure narrative. NO victory lap. Humility + physical act.
- **Genre**: Kenshi Yonezu / LOSER-leaning — bass-driven groove, melody-first, ~92 BPM, D minor.
- **Emotional temperature**: exhausted, relieved, uncertain, but moving forward. NOT angry. Melancholic but moving.
- **Directives from audit**: PRESERVE "Shenzhen North" and "cold bright mouth" (Cursor Pre-Chorus). LESS polish, MORE raw. Let the song breathe — not every line needs to "perform."
