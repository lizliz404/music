# PART 1 — CONCISE AUDIT

## 1. Untapped source images

Across the earlier lyrics quoted in the audit and v3, none used these:

1. Moving the mattress and discovering a noise source on every side.
2. Jets overhead, daily trains, road traffic, and construction steel converging on one room.
3. Walking through a crowd with the phone almost touching the mouth, humming because the melody might disappear.
4. Paper books weighing down a migrant suitcase—knowledge becoming a physical anchor.
5. Thirteen hundred yuan left behind for fifteen days of livable time; laughing and crying over the calculation.
6. VIP lanes glowing beside ordinary station queues; advertising multiplying wherever people gather.
7. The shopping-app chime that the child recognizes before anyone explains it.
8. “Homework” traveling from balcony to balcony through the old residential compound.
9. Rooftop wind carrying the distant river singer across the city.

## 2. v3 quality

- Hook — **9.2/10**  
  The broken grammar remains faithful to the seed. The loose Interlude finally reproduces its repetition, fillers, and failing language rather than merely quoting the hook.

- Imagery — **8.6/10**  
  “Cold bright mouth,” cement through the ribs, the black pan, childhood clay, and the face-down phone are strong. “Morning grey,” “same old links,” “barely right,” and “corridor is dead” are comparatively generic or strained.

- Authenticity — **8.1/10**  
  The physical and domestic details feel lived. But v3 relocates the fifteen-year stall into Verse 1 before returning it home in Verse 2, and “same man’s hands” is unsupported invention. If the stated 06:19 UTC timestamp is accurate, the recording occurred around 14:19 in Shenzhen, making “morning grey” doubtful.

- Source utilization — **7.2/10**  
  It uses the most obvious source images but misses the actual birth of the song: luggage in motion, phone at the mouth, strangers passing, the layered transport noise, the deposit calculation, and the contradictory physical burden of books.

- Overall — **8.3/10**

## 3. Maximized or leaner?

It got **leaner, not maximized**. V3 successfully performed the prior audit’s three surgeries—fragmented Interlude, “and that’s enough,” and removal of weaker abstractions—but mostly consolidated existing material.

Still missing:

- The embodied composition scene: walking, wheels catching seams, phone held close, people flowing around the singer.
- The room’s complete acoustic siege rather than only the cement truck.
- The financial residue of leaving: the key, deposit, and fifteen-day trade.
- Luggage as more than a prop—especially books becoming weight.
- More station-specific social texture: VIP lane and advertising walls.
- Home rendered through involuntary sounds rather than conclusions about belonging.
- A cleaner chronology and a Bridge whose wound, question, and action are unmistakably separate.
