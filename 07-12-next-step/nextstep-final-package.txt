PART 2: SUNO STYLE PROMPTS — A/B
═══════════════════════════════════════════

Both prompts assume Custom Mode (Style + Lyrics fields separate).
Always use the Exclude Styles field — do NOT put excludes in the Style field.

─── Style A: The Elegy ───

Style prompt:
Intimate bass-led chamber art-rock, restrained pocket drums, whisper-close controlled male vocal, warm acoustic guitar and felt piano, subtle strings with cold synth undertow, slow-bloom dynamics, spacious polished mix, 92 BPM D minor

Production notes:
- Interlude: Near-ASMR vocal, dry and breath-close, suspended over faint bass and luggage-wheel texture. No drums. Let the fillers (hmm/oh/yeah) breathe.
- Outro: Strip to voice only. Remove drums and harmony. Isolated piano notes. Near-silence by the final "and that's enough."
- Overall: Vulnerability-first. The exhaustion IS the production value — don't polish it away.

Exclude Styles:
pop, radio polish, autotune, trap, EDM, synth-pop, dream-pop, pop-punk, emo, industrial, grunge, shouting, screaming, lo-fi, garage rock, heavy distortion, guitar solos, rough production

Creative Sliders:
| Slider       | Setting | Reason |
|--------------|---------|--------|
| Weirdness    | 30-40   | Stable hook, let the intimacy carry novelty |
| Style Inf.   | 70-80   | Tight adherence — Kenshi DNA must not drift to pop |
| Audio Inf.   | —       | Not using audio upload for this style |

─── Style B: The Departure ───

Style prompt:
Propulsive melodic art-rock, bassline-hook groove, tight syncopated drums, precise controlled male vocal, abrupt hard section cuts, cold digital synth and warm guitar tension, transit-percussion textures, clean punchy mix at 92 BPM D minor

Production notes:
- Interlude: Fragment the groove beneath a filtered station announcement, passing-train noise, and murmured vocal layers. Not a breakdown — a texture shift. The song keeps moving underneath.
- Outro: Strip harmony but retain muted bass-and-kick pulse. Add luggage-wheel rhythm as percussion. Stark, unresolved, still moving. The train hasn't stopped.
- Overall: Groove as emotional engine — not aggression, propulsion. Section contrast tells the transit story.

Exclude Styles:
pop, radio polish, autotune, trap hi-hats, EDM drops, choir pads, synth-pop, dream-pop, pop-punk, emo, industrial, grunge, shouting, screaming, lo-fi, garage rock, excessive distortion, long solos, aggression over melody

PART 3: PRODUCTION ADDENDA (from suno-production skill)
═══════════════════════════════════════════

Custom Mode: REQUIRED. Style field + Lyrics field + Exclude Styles field must all be populated separately.

Hybrid Model Workflow (recommended for v5.5):
- Generate structure on v4.5 or v5 → Cover on v5.5 for vocal quality
- Why: v4.5/v5 gives stable structure without mid-track degradation. Cover on v5.5 inherits stronger vocals WITHOUT pop reversion or unreliable Extend.
- For the Interlude specifically: generate the Interlude section as part of the full track, NOT as an Extend — the murmuring quality needs the full structural context.

Tag Economy Check:
- Style A: 8 tags ✅ (sweet spot: 5-8)
- Style B: 8 tags ✅
- Both: ~350 chars ✅ (under Suno's silent truncation threshold)
- No artist names ✅ (Suno ignores them entirely — we used sonic descriptors instead)

Pop Gravity Well Escape:
- Both prompts lead with "art-rock" NOT "pop" or "rock"
- Both explicitly exclude: pop, pop-punk, emo, radio polish
- Key: Suno's default drift is toward pop. Without excludes, 60-70% of generations land there.
- Additional safeguard: if tracks drift toward generic pop hooks, raise Style Influence to 80-85.

Structure Tags (for Lyrics field in Custom Mode):
- Keep tags SPARSE. They're hints, not code.
- Chorus lyrics MUST be IDENTICAL on every repeat — Suno rewards repetition for hook recognition.
- For Interlude: [Interlude] tag on its own line. Do NOT add vocal performance tags — let the loose/murmured direction come from the style prompt.
- For Outro: [Outro — slowing, almost spoken] on its own line.
- End with [End] (clean stop) or [Fade Out] (trail-off). Recommend [Fade Out] for both styles.

Credit Efficiency:
- Stop after 2-4 generations per style. Listen. Score. Choose ONE.
- Don't chase the "perfect" take in generation — use Replace Section for problem spots.
- Blind regeneration loops = biggest credit waste.

3-Listen Test:
1st: Gut. Hook. Did anything make me want to hear it again?
2nd: Structure. Does the song move like a song, not a loop? Is the Interlude distinct?
3rd: Weak sections. Can problems be fixed with ONE control move (Replace Section, Extend, Crop)?
