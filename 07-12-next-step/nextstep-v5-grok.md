# PART 2 — “NEXT STEP” v5

**[Verse 1]**

Eleventh floor, the glass still trembling
Cement truck rolling through my ribs at eighty
Three months in a box where the walls stayed thin
The neighbor’s cough, the late-night driver scrolling
I moved the mattress — every side had sound
Paperbacks pulled hard against the zipper seam
Left the key where the landlord said
Thirteen hundred stayed behind when I left
Phone close to my mouth, wheels knocking every seam
People stream around me; I keep humming low

**[Pre-Chorus]**

The station lights cut through the afternoon haze
Shenzhen North opens like a cold bright mouth
A family of four, four screens, no words to say
Volunteer vest blurs past — I drag my weight
One hand on the handle, one hand on my chest

**[Chorus]**

I don’t even know
I don’t even know
I don’t even know where’s next step
The platform hums, the timetable blinks
I can’t go back through those same old links
I don’t even know
I don’t even know where’s next step

**[Verse 2]**

Back home, the same stall fries dough at the corner stand
Fifteen years — same black pan same arches
Father at the table, same soft line —
“Chew slow, hold the bowl” — a decade, same refrain
Brother’s phone eats half his face in light
A shopping chime; he says, “Go grab the coins”
I sit between them like I’m barely there
Through the window, “homework” moves balcony to balcony
At dusk, the river singer reaches the roof
The muddy lake road from the house I knew
Childhood clay still underneath my shoe
I can visit home but I can’t stay long
The hallway knows my footsteps don’t belong

**[Bridge]**

Something tore — I can’t remember what
Maybe it was the ceiling that forgot
The mixer still turns somewhere in my ribs

Thin walls still ringing in my ears

Am I walking toward something
or only not going back?

I set the phone face-down and lift the bag
The handle clicks
My left foot takes the weight
