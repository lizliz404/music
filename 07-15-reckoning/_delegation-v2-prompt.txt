## WHAT V2 MUST ADD (mine the research)

### From Clark & Mills: The Norm-Violation Mechanism
The calculator doesn't just lose in the long run — they DAMAGE relationships in real time. When someone detects they're being "accounted for," attraction drops immediately. The core wound is not "I calculated wrong" but "I misclassified the entire game — applied exchange norms to communal relationships." This is a sharper, more precise regret. Use it.

### From the Bootstrap Problem
Any trust relationship requires an "irrational" first step — uncollateralized cooperation on Round 1. The calculator can never take that step because they need ROI data before investing, but ROI data can only be generated by investing first. They are stuck at Round 0. This is a powerful image: standing at the edge of every relationship, unable to step in.

### From Costly Signaling
The calculator CANNOT produce a credible costly signal. Their transparency ("I optimize everything") makes their loyalty non-credible — the other party observes they will switch the moment a better option appears. The cost of the signal is the DELIBERATE REFUSAL TO ACCOUNT. The calculator is incapable of not-tracking. This is the mechanism of their isolation.

### Rhyme & Prosody
- Mix perfect and slant rhymes. All perfect = nursery rhyme. All slant = lazy.
- Prioritize singability: say it out loud. If you stumble, fix it.
- Unstable feelings → near-rhymes / wandering melodies. Resolution → perfect rhymes.

### Bridge — 3 Moves Only
1. **One wound** — a specific memory/origin image (not a metaphor)
2. **One question** — raising the agency stake
3. **One act** — a physical decision (door closes, stands up, light on, cup set down)

- No "I choose the harder side," no "that's enough," no victory lap
- Use: humility ("Not cured. Not pure."), partial progress, physical act as resolution
- The narrator is standing in a doorway. They haven't arrived. They've only stopped calculating.
