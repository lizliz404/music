Write an all-English song titled "Reckoning" (or a better title you derive).

## Concept

A person who has optimized every relationship into transactions — ROI, cost/benefit, efficiency — becomes an "optimization machine." The song is the moment of reckoning: realizing what's been lost. The thing that can't be calculated. The sense of belonging.

## Core Tension

Transactional logic vs genuine belonging. The narrator has been treating people like ledger entries. Now they feel the hollow — and they can describe what they've lost through the negative space of what 周耀辉 captured in 《老伴》:

"有没有 春花秋月 夏蝉冬雪 不会散去
有没有 一关上门 一躺下来 不再离去
有没有 爱看的天 爱踏的地
我爱着的你 陪我美丽的老去"

Translation of the feeling (NOT to be used as lyrics — adapt the IMAGERY):
Is there spring flowers autumn moon summer cicadas winter snow that won't scatter?
Is there closing the door, lying down, and not leaving again?
Is there a sky I love to watch, ground I love to walk?
You whom I love, growing old beautifully with me.

That's belonging. That's what the efficiency machine can't buy, can't optimize, can't calculate. The song should make the listener FEEL the cost, not just hear it stated.

## Source Materials

1. The philosophical frame: "如果所有关系都只按价值计算，或者说人百分百遵循价值社交的逻辑，那他会变成什么样？性价比、投产比如何？风险和代价是什么？效率优化机器？" — What happens when someone treats all relationships as pure value calculation? What are the costs and risks? They become an efficiency optimization machine.

2. 周耀辉's lyrics above — the counter-image of what genuine belonging feels like.

## Voice & Craft

- All English lyrics. No Chinese.
- Show concrete scenes, not thesis statements. Images over verdicts.
- The narrator should feel the LOSS, not just name it. Let the audience reach the conclusion.
- Use transactional/accounting imagery (ledger, balance, calculate, optimize, return, cost) as the vocabulary of the efficiency machine — then let it crack.
- The bridge should change the TERMS of the song — one wound, one question, one physical act.

- Vocal delivery: controlled, sung, melodic. NO screaming, shouting, belting, or "raw" vocals.
- Melody-first, rich arrangement, distinct sections.
