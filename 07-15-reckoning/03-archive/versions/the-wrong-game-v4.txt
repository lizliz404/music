# The Wrong Game — v4 (Grok 4.5, surgical, 2026-07-15)

[Intro: sparse electric guitar motif, close-mic hush, no drums yet]

[Verse 1: hushed, close-mic, melodic, sparse piano]
I met you in a doorway I could never cross,
ran the figures twice before I'd say hello.
I needed proof of profit, needed proof of loss,
some quarterly return before I'd let it grow.
So I stood at the threshold with my numbers dry,
waiting on a track record that could not begin —
you can't earn the interest till you've made the buy,
I'd never make the buy, so I never stepped in.

[Chorus: intense, controlled, full band, incisive delivery]
I priced you like a stranger while you held me like a friend,
and the second that you felt it, something quiet turned and left.
It was never that the math came out too thin —
it's that you knew, from the counting, I had never let you in.
Round one asks a fool to give with nothing back to hold;
I ran the numbers till the doorway emptied out.
Not the wrong sum. The wrong game.
I was solving for a stranger. You were spelling out my name.

[Verse 2: denser, controlled, rhythmic pulse, tight drums]
I can't shut down — a server through the night —
the moment I stop earning, every signal starts to fade.
I won't lie down beside you, won't turn off the light;
the ledger writes me down the moment I lie still.
I built a wide bright network, not a single root goes deep,
a mile of useful faces and no hand to hold my own.
I selected for the profit, then the profit selected me —
alone among the assets with the network running blank.

[Chorus: intense, controlled, full band, incisive delivery]
You gave without a receipt and I gave to be repaid,
and you saw it in the giving — how I flinched, how I appraised.
It was never that I chose the colder side —
it's that I could not stop the counting long enough to let it slide.
The proof of what I'd stay for is refusing to keep score,
but not-keeping-score's the one thing that I never learned to do.
Not the wrong sum. The wrong game.
No seasons, just the quarters — and a number, and no you.

[Bridge: stripped to voice and guitar, intimate, fragile]
On the grass that summer, before I had the words,
I asked a friend to letter my name on a paper crane, and didn't ask what for.
He wrote it crooked. I put it in my pocket. That was the whole exchange —
no rate, no return, just ink and paper and the grass.
So who stays for a man who can't stop taking inventory?
Who lies down and won't get up to leave when the value's low?
I don't have the answer. I have a pen, a page, a night.
I closed the book. I didn't tally. I let one gift just be a gift.

[Final Chorus: peak intensity, controlled, denser arrangement, strings enter]
I priced you like a stranger while you held me like a friend,
and the second that you felt it, something quiet turned and left.
It was never that the math came out too thin —
it's that you knew, from the counting, I had never let you in.
I can't unlearn the counting in a night —
I only put the ledger down and did not pick it back up.
Not cured. Not pure. Just a hand that won't compute,
one uncollateralized reach across the dark.
I'm standing where I couldn't stand before —
in the doorway, not calculating, with my hand against the door.

[Outro: guitar motif returns, strings exit, voice fades close-mic]
Not the wrong sum. The wrong game.
