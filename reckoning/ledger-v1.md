# Ledger — v1 (Opus 4.8)

[Verse 1]
I priced you out the morning that we met,
ran the numbers, filed you under net.
Your laughter had a yield, your time a rate,
I logged each favor owed and made you wait.
I kept a ledger where a heart should be,
every hello a line of receivable.
I called it wisdom, called it staying free —
a clean account and nothing left to feel.

[Chorus]
But there's no column for the way you hummed,
no line item for the porch light left on.
You can't invoice the smell of coming home,
can't optimize the hand that holds your own.
I balanced every book and lost the room,
the door that stayed unlocked, the empty chair —
I calculated everything to zero,
and zero is the sound of no one there.

[Verse 2]
I traded Sunday mornings for the gain,
sold the slow ones, kept the ones who paid.
I streamlined every friend into a name,
a cost-per-hour, a debt to be repaid.
The dinners ran on schedule, then on mute,
I cut the guests who couldn't clear the bar.
Efficient as a blade and just as mute —
I got so good I don't know where you are.

[Chorus]
'Cause there's no column for the way you stayed,
no metric for the rain against the glass.
You can't discount a season we let pass,
can't liquidate the years we thought would last.
I balanced every book and lost the room,
the sky I used to watch, the ground I'd walk —
I calculated everything to zero,
and zero doesn't answer when I talk.

[Bridge]
There's a cup in the cabinet that isn't mine.
I keep it. I don't know why I keep it.
Tonight I set it down and let it sit,
and the quiet asked me one thing, plain and slow:
who would grow old with a machine like me,
who'd close the door and choose to not leave?
I don't have the answer. I have my two hands.
So I turned off the ledger. I left the light on.

[Chorus]
There's no column for the years I want back,
no interest earned on all the nights alone.
I can't refund the warmth I called a waste —
but I washed the cup, and I set it down at home.
I can't rebuild the room I priced away,
I only know the door, and it's unlocked —
I stopped the count somewhere around your name,
and I'm standing in the doorway. And I'll wait.
