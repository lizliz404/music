# "Next Step" v5 — Style I: California Hills
## Eagles × 李宗盛 fusion
## BPM/Key: 92 BPM, D minor

### Concept
取《Hotel California》的加州暖调摇滚骨架——双吉他交织对话、模拟录音的宽阔声场、不急于进副歌的叙事型结构；取《山丘》念白式的低沉男声、留白大于填充的吐字节奏、将情感重量交给语气而非音量的克制。两者相遇，让这首歌在加州暖调摇滚的器乐包裹中保持李宗盛式的平实叙事——不愤怒，不感伤，只是在走。

### Style Prompt
```
70s California rock, conversational folk-rock, dual clean electric guitars weaving harmonies, warm analog live-room production, baritone talk-singing narrative vocal, unhurried cinematic phrasing, Hammond organ subtle pads, brushed drums
```

### Exclude Styles

```
pop, pop-punk, emo, radio polish, autotune, trap, EDM, synth-pop, dream-pop, belting, shouting, screaming, lo-fi, garage rock, excessive distortion, guitar shredding, arena rock, hard rock, country rock, digital production, four-on-the-floor, electronic drums
```

### Sliders
Weirdness: 30 · Style Influence: 75

### Production Notes
- **Interlude treatment**: Strip to one clean guitar arpeggio and voice. The murmured/loose delivery is the emotional center — push talk-singing to its most intimate extreme. Dual guitars enter only as harmonic ghosts (volume-pedal swells, barely-there harmonics). Near-ASMR proximity — the listener should hear breath between phrases.
- **Outro treatment**: Dual guitars fade one at a time. By "One step more," only one guitar remains — clean, unhurried, no reverb wash. Final "and that's enough" lands on voice alone, or voice + a single sustained guitar harmonic that decays to silence. No fade-out — a deliberate ending.
- **Guitar solo / instrumental break**: Hotel California DNA demands a dual-guitar moment. Place an instrumental break after the Bridge (before the Interlude), 8-16 bars. The two clean electric guitars call and respond — not shredding, not showing off — weaving a conversation that echoes the song's central question: *walking toward something, or only not going back?* The lead guitar should carry melodic weight without ever overpowering the narrative frame. Let the Interlude that follows feel like the exhale after that exchange.
