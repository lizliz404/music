## Song Architecture

### Genre Direction — Kenshi Yonezu / LOSER-leaning
- Melody first, bass-driven groove engine
- Sung, not shouted — controlled, precise, incisive
- Clean, polished, punchy mix
- Male vocals, rhythmic and melodic
- BPM ~92, minor key (D minor suggested)

### Section Guidance
- **Chorus**: "I don't even know" must anchor the hook. The title phrase lands on the downbeat.
- **Verses**: Concrete images from the source — train station, screen zombies, 水泥车共振, the rental room, dragging luggage, 公婆饼摊, the family dinner table
- **Bridge**: 3 moves only — one specific wound/memory, one question raising the stakes, one physical act/decision
- **Ending**: NO cure narrative. No "I choose the harder side." Use humility: "Not cured. Not pure." Partial progress. Physical act as resolution.

## Craft Rules (from Liz's preferences)

### Vocal Delivery — ABSOLUTE BAN
NO: screaming, shouting, belting, roaring, "explosive," "raw," rough/lo-fi
YES: controlled, sung, melodic, incisive, precise, intimate, close-mic

### Images Over Verdicts
Show a scene, don't state the thesis. Concrete sensory images:
- RIGHT: "fifteen years, the same three starches frying in the same black pan"
- WRONG: "he's not the villain — he's the system's door"

### Unified Metaphor Axis
Commit to ONE metaphor system (journey/movement, or transit, or weight/gravity). Don't mix.

### Bridge — 3 Moves Only
1. One wound — specific memory or origin image
2. One question — raising the agency stake  
3. One act — physical decision (door closes, stands up, phone face down)

### Ending
NO victory laps. Use: humility, partial progress, physical act as resolution.
No "I woke up and everything changed."

### Breathing & Density
- Speech patterns (rapid-fire, frantic) → keep tight
- Reflective observations → give white space

## Emotional Arc
The song should trace: leaving a bad place → the relief + uncertainty of the in-between → the weight of where you came from → the decision to keep walking despite not knowing

The emotional temperature is NOT angry. It's exhausted, clear-eyed, melancholic but moving. Think: someone who just survived something and is now standing in a train station, luggage in hand, looking at the departures board, knowing they can't go back but not sure where they're going.
