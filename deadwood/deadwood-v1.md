# DEADWOOD (废柴) — v1

**Status**: v1 (first pass off IMPOTENT lineage)
**Date**: 2026-07-12
**Author**: Liz (design brief / audit v5) / execution: Cursor
**Language**: English
**Base**: IMPOTENT v4 (final) + audit v5 metaphor swap
**Key**: D minor · ~92 BPM
**Genre**: Melodic art-rock, Kenshi-core

> **Title wordplay**: Chinese slang *废柴* (useless wood) and English corporate *deadwood*
> (redundant personnel to cut) converged on the same metaphor across two languages —
> not translation, coincidence. A Kenshi-style title trick (M87/M78 lineage).
>
> **Core reversal**: *deadwood* is not society's verdict on a failure. It's the **analyzer-self's**
> label for the **actor-self** — the part willing to publish, be seen, hear "no." Analysis stays
> "complete" because it's never rejected; the acting self gets branded *waste* to justify the safe zone.
> Dry deadwood is also the best kindling — what gets called useless is the most flammable.

## Lyrics

```
[Intro]
(Clean, precise — solo voice, cold synth pad underneath, glitch textures
and phone-scroll clicks fade in. ~4 bars solo, then band locks in, ~8 bars total.
This is "the counter" — the analyzer's voice.)

Soft glow, soft glow —
all light and no heat.

[Verse 1]
[Hushed, close-mic, melodic — sparse bass pulse, ambient pad.
Let the glow feel good for a few bars before it turns. Sung, not spoken.
Still the counter's voice.]

Three a.m. and the glow feels good,
no one's calling, nothing's due —
I let the feed love me first,
one more frame, then just one more.

They packed a lifetime into two hours flat —
love, and war, and the big reveal,
I felt it all from right where I sat.
Out there it takes years to arrive.
In here I tap and it's mine tonight.

Somewhere I stopped tasting it —
I look up — the water's gone cold,
three hours gone and the cup never moved.
Reality tastes so thin
after all that I consumed.

[Pre-Chorus 1]
[Drums enter soft, bass builds underneath.
Pull the tempo feel back — half-time, holding.]

Junk food heaven, and it's not a lie —
just not the meal that keeps me alive.
No score, no cut, no easy blaze —
just an ordinary, unlit day.

[Chorus 1]
[Hard cut — NO transition. Drums snap to double-time drive, bass-forward.
Distorted guitars, full band. Controlled, sung — power through precision, not volume.
The counter names the verdict.]

DEADWOOD —
[one beat rest]
that's the verdict.
The part of me that's ready to burn,
they cross it out, they call it waste.

DEADWOOD —
warming my hands on a fire I didn't light,
and calling it enough —
just one more scroll,
just one more.

[Verse 2]
[More urgent, energy pulled back slightly — still melodic, sung.
The counter turns on itself.]

I know this script — I know it cold.

[one beat silence]

Still using a wrecked brain
to examine its own wreckage —

[one beat silence]

that's the cruelest loop I own.

Someone drew this, aimed this, built it to catch —
and it caught me, one scroll at a time.
Same brain that grades the rest of me "not yet"
is the one that keeps the match away.

[Pre-Chorus 2]
[Building, more urgency. Half-time hold again before the cut.]

Some days it's quieter than the highlight reel,
some days I can't tell what's even real.
So ask it straight when the credits roll:
does this warm me through, or leave me cold?

[Chorus 2]
[Same hard cut into double-time. Denser — layered vocals, backing harmonies.
Same shape as Chorus 1; one line deepens instead of resolving.
Intensity from layering, not volume.]

DEADWOOD —
[one beat rest]
that's the verdict.
I know the label's wrong, and still I sign it.

DEADWOOD —
warming my hands on a fire I didn't light,
and calling it enough —
just one more scroll, I know, one more.

[Bridge]
[Strip to piano or clean guitar, vulnerable.
The counter and the igniter trade lines, about to merge.]

I was seventeen,
thumb deep in a feed of strangers,
and I froze —
is this my whole life — this glow?

[Full band locks in on this line — clean, precise, no distortion wash.
This is the action; both voices at once.]

I put the phone face down.

[Pull back — reflective aftermath, the igniter's voice now]

Am I just kindling for somebody's feed,
or — at least — the one who strikes the match?

[Brief Instrumental Break]
(Melodic, tight — 4 bars max, serves the song, doesn't wander. Bass leads.)

[Final Chorus]
[Peak intensity, controlled — then receding for the last lines.
The igniter reclaims the word. Still sung. Power from arrangement density, not vocal force.]

DEADWOOD —
[one beat rest]
fine — then deadwood catches first.
Not a saint, not a cynic either —
just dry wood deciding to burn.

Crowd the dark out with a page, a line, a song —
make the kind of light you have to feed.

Not cured. Not pure.
Not lit all at once —
some days I still lose. That's the start.

[Outro]
[Stripped — voice and reverb, sparse piano.
Back to the counter's quiet, but changed.]

Soft glow, soft glow —
the phone stays face down.
Water's cold, the room's gone quiet.
The fire's not loud,
but it's mine to feed now.

[End]
```

## Changelog — from IMPOTENT v4

**Metaphor system swap (impotence/阳痿 → deadwood/枯木/引火):**
- **Title**: IMPOTENT → DEADWOOD (废柴). Bilingual convergence wordplay as a Kenshi-style title trick.
- **Core reversal**: "deadwood" is the analyzer-self's label for the actor-self (the one who ships, is seen, hears "no"). Analysis feels "complete" because it's never rejected; the acting self gets branded waste to keep the safe zone.
- **Chorus hook rebuilt**: `IMPOTENT — toward reality / dopamine on loan, bill comes due` → `DEADWOOD — that's the verdict / warming my hands on a fire I didn't light`. Consuming others' creation = staying unlit.
- **Final chorus reclaims the insult (LOSER move)**: `fine — then deadwood catches first` — dry deadwood is the best kindling; what's called useless is the most flammable.
- **Single axis enforced**: all competing metaphors purged — economics gone (`dopamine on loan`, `bill comes due`, `bought back`, `current for somebody's meter`, `night on loan`). One axis now: dead wood / dry timber / kindling / spark / catch / burn / feed. Phone `glow` recast as *false fire* (all light, no heat), folding into the fire axis rather than a rival light metaphor.

**Two surgical edits (audit v5):**
1. **Verse 2** — "Still using a wrecked brain to examine its own wreckage" isolated with a beat of silence before and after. It's the epistemological core (the analyzer analyzing itself is *why* analysis feels safe), previously buried.
2. **Bridge** — "I put the phone face down" moved to directly after "is this my whole life — this glow?" as an **action-answer**. "Am I just kindling…" becomes reflective aftermath, not a second question.

**Preserved (both source audits agreed):**
- `I look up — the water's gone cold, three hours gone and the cup never moved` — verbatim.
- `Still using a wrecked brain to examine its own wreckage — that's the cruelest loop I own` — verbatim.
- `I put the phone face down` — verbatim (relocated per edit 2).
- `Soft glow, soft glow` — intro/outro bookend, verbatim.
- `Not cured. Not pure. … some days I still lose. That's the start.` — kept; only `bought back` → `lit` to hold the single fire axis.

**Kenshi production (kept + sharpened):**
- **Dual-voice (M87)**: "the counter" (analyzer) vs "the igniter" (actor), marked in section tags, merging at the bridge action line.
- **Roller-coaster (KICK BACK)**: half-time pre-choruses hard-cut into double-time choruses, no smoothing.
- **Persona-split (Flamingo)** reframed: analyzer vs actor, not star vs nobody.
- Bass-forward engine, cold-synth/warm-guitar tension, controlled sung vocals (no scream/shout/belt), ~92 BPM, D minor.

**Theme note:** per audit v5, center of gravity shifts off "phone/dopamine addiction" (a saturated 2020s topic) onto the analyze-vs-ship split. The phone/feed remains as the emblem of consuming-not-creating, not the subject itself.
