## Creative Brief

**Core concept**: The dopamine trap — how supernormal stimuli (fiction, games, porn, short videos) raise your threshold until real life feels flat and impotent. The song moves from confession → recognition → a hard-won turn toward production over consumption.

**Key metaphors from source essay**:
- "Impotence toward reality" — the title hook
- "Consumption is borrowing, production is compounding"
- "Squeeze out the bad with the good"
- Red pill / Matrix / brain in a vat
- "Those inside can't get out, those outside don't need this" (fortress metaphor)
- Junk food as supernormal stimulus
- "Knowing, but unable to act" — the real terror

**Emotional arc**:
Intro (numb, static) → V1 (confession, hollow) → Pre (dawning awareness) → Chorus (gut-punch, cathartic) → V2 (deeper trap, self-awareness paradox) → Pre → Chorus (bigger) → Bridge (teenage memory → defiant turn) → Guitar Solo (raw release) → Final Chorus (resolution, not hopeless) → Outro (quiet, earned)

**Genre**: Alternative rock / post-punk with electronic textures
**Tempo**: ~88-92 BPM
**Key**: D minor
**Vocal**: Male, weathered/raspy, intimate whisper building to explosive belted chorus
