## v3 Changelog (from v2)

### Bridge — the big surgery
- **Cut**: red pill ceremony, full scientists room, city walls couplet, loan/compound math
- **Kept**: seventeen freeze → player/current question → physical decision (phone face down)
- **Why**: audit correctly identified bridge as metaphor dump. Now it's three moves: one wound, one question, one act.

### IMPOTENT landing — protected
- Both chorus entrances now carry `[one beat rest]` after the title word
- Prevents the word from being sung into the following clause as one breath — it now lands as a musical event, not a thesis statement.

### Junk food restored, weak Pre-Chorus replaced
- Moved junk-food ambivalence into Pre-Chorus 1 ("Junk food heaven, and it's not a lie — just not the meal that keeps me alive")
- Replaced the audit-flagged prose line "Nothing's broken about the ordinary day" with concrete imagery

### Chorus 2 finally differs from Chorus 1
- Kept the real hook intact: "dopamine on loan... just one more"
- Swapped the first half of C1 to "I know the cost, and still I pay it" — naming the essay's core terror ("knowing but unable to act") directly rather than only implying it

### Scientists demoted from scene to whisper
- Reduced from a full bridge scene to one oblique line in V2: "Someone built this, aimed it, shipped it, sold it — and I bought it, one scroll at a time"
- Priority clarified: the seventeen memory is your own; the scientists are borrowed (Naval). They don't compete for the same bridge real estate.

### Two missing essay beats recovered
- **The glow feeling good**: V1 now lets the feed feel pleasurable for four lines before it turns hollow — the moral arc isn't pre-scripted
- **Loneliness**: "no one's calling, nothing's due" — a single line acknowledging the relational vacuum that makes the screen seductive
- **"Some days" foreshadowed**: Pre-Chorus 2 now carries "some days it's quieter" / "some days I can't tell" — the ending's humility no longer arrives unannounced

### Ending skeleton preserved, anti-cynicism restored
- "Not cured. Not pure" stays
- Grok's "not a saint, not a cynic either" restored — the essay explicitly warns against the sneering-from-above posture; losing this line was a quiet loss

## Suno Style Prompt (v3)

```
Alternative rock, post-punk revival, male vocals raw and weathered,
one-word shouted title hook with a full beat of silence after it,
intimate close-mic dry-room verses, dynamic arc from hushed near-whisper
verse to explosive belted chorus and back to a hush in the outro,
anthemic chorus without stacked choir pads, distorted electric guitars
and driving live drums, cold digital synth pads sitting underneath warm
tape-saturated guitars kept distinct not blended, bridge stripped to
voice and piano before the full band returns on the decision line, one
raw pentatonic-blues guitar solo after the bridge, 92 BPM, D minor,
moody and melancholic but defiant, singable anthemic hooks without
overproduction, English lyrics only
```

**Exclude**: `pop, mainstream radio polish, autotune, trap hi-hats, EDM drops, choir pad spam, synth-pop, spoken-word only, Chinese vocals, neon/dream-pop atmosphere`

---

## Open Decision Point

**Verse 2 line: "Someone built this, aimed it, shipped it, sold it —"**

Two tonal options, Liz's call:
- **Subtle** (current): almost undetectable as industry critique — the listener hears a vague systemic force, not a named antagonist. Keeps the bedroom-confession intimacy.
- **More explicit**: e.g. "A room of strangers built this, aimed it, sold it —" — listeners would catch the "algorithm vs me" framing. Trades intimacy for political bite.

Audit said: "that's a taste/tone call for Liz, not a craft call."

---

## Production Note

Cold synth pads = the algorithm / engineered desire. Warm tape-saturated guitars = the real self. The arrangement fights itself the way the lyrics do. In the bridge, warm wins — the band returns on "I put the phone face down."
