## Synthesis Notes

v2 merges the best of v1 (Lyric/DeepSeek) and v2-grok (Grok 4.5) with three critical corrections:

1. **Recovered v1's two strongest images** — "using a wrecked brain to examine its own wreckage" and "I was seventeen, thumb deep in a feed of strangers, and I froze." These are the rawest, most specific images in the source material and v2-grok dropped them entirely.

2. **Added the external antagonist** — Naval's "a room full of scientists built this hunger, aimed it well." This transforms the song from pure introspection ("me vs myself") into "individual vs designed system," doubling the dramatic tension.

3. **Fixed the ending** — v1's "I choose the harder side / that's enough" read like a victory lap, contradicting the essay's own warning about the perfectionism trap. v2 adopts Grok's "not cured, not pure" humility but adds "some days. That's the start" — no one-shot cure fantasy.

4. **BPM settled at 92** — not a compromise for its own sake. The whisper-to-roar dynamic arc needs breathing room; 88 was slightly sluggish, 100 rushed the quiet sections. 92 gives space without dragging.

5. **Production metaphor** — Cold synth pads = algorithm / engineered desire. Warm guitar and raw vocals = the real self. The arrangement should fight itself the way the lyrics do.

## Suno Style Prompt

```
Alternative rock, post-punk revival, male vocals raw and weathered,
dynamic arc from hushed near-whisper verse to explosive belted chorus
and back to a hush in the outro, distorted electric guitars and driving
live drums under a cold undertow of cinematic synth pads, sparse piano
and clean guitar in the bridge building into one raw pentatonic-blues
guitar solo, tape warmth on the live instruments set against a colder
digital texture on the pads, 92 BPM, D minor, moody and melancholic
but defiant, singable anthemic hooks without overproduction,
English lyrics only
```

**Exclude**: `pop, mainstream radio polish, autotune, trap hi-hats, EDM drops, choir pad spam, synth-pop, spoken-word only, Chinese vocals`

---

## Production Note — Sonic Metaphor

The arrangement should encode the song's core conflict:
- **Cold synth pads** = the algorithm, engineered desire, the "room full of scientists"
- **Warm guitars and raw vocals** = the real self, the stubborn body pushing back

Don't blend them into a unified "post-punk texture." Let them fight. The cold should feel colder against the warmth, and vice versa. The bridge is where the warm side wins.
