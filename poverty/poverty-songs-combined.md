## Lyrics

```
[Intro]
(One guitar, fingerpicked, slightly out of tune — or a single piano note held)

There is a bookshelf in Hengyang
covered in dust
and Jane Eyre
in two languages.

[Verse 1]
[Spoken-sung, close-mic, conversational]

My mother had handwriting so small and neat
she could fit a whole dictionary in the margins.
Le Rouge et le Noir. Jane Eyre. The spines cracked,
pages yellow at the edges like old teeth.
She sat up straight when she read —
I know because I've reconstructed it from the ruins.

Same woman. Same hands. Same eyes.
Thirty years later, those eyes are hungry now
for a different kind of screen —
Pinduoduo. Bargain countdown. Free shipping threshold.
Come help me cut the price, she says.
Just one more person. Just one more click.
The literary girl went somewhere
and never came back. I don't know
if she died or just got too tired to sit up straight.

[Pre-Chorus]
[Drums enter — simple, driving]

I tell myself: this is just a specimen.
A data point. A case study.
I tell myself: the distance keeps you clean.

[Chorus]
[Explosive — distorted, raw, almost shouted]

Quiet costs money.
Quiet costs money and I don't have enough yet.
The cesspool doesn't need renovation —
it needs you to climb out.
Silence is not the default setting —
you have to pay for every decibel you lose.
I'm saving up.

[Verse 2]
[Pulled back — grittier]

My father is a machine gun loaded with small things.
Trivialities. Complaints about the water pressure,
the way the rice was cooked, the neighbour's dog,
the government, the weather, the way I hold my spoon.
He fires in all directions. He has been firing
since before I was born. The ammunition is infinite —
it's made of nothing, so it never runs out.

Mornings: refined flour, fried in oil,
cheapest dopamine money can buy.
He calls it breakfast. I call it
a carbohydrate funeral for the part of the brain
that might have wanted something different.
But the body takes what it can get
when the rest of the day is just more of the same.

[Pre-Chorus]
[Building]

I tell myself: he's a systemic failure case.
An API through which the system applies pressure.
I tell myself: the analysis protects you.

[Chorus]
[Bigger, more desperation]

Quiet costs money.
Quiet costs money and I don't have enough yet.
The cesspool doesn't need renovation —
it needs you to climb out.
Silence is not the default setting —
you have to pay for every decibel you lose.
I'm saving up.

[Verse 3]
[Stripped back — just voice and sparse guitar/piano]

Two yuan fifty. A roasted sweet potato.
University canteen. I stood at the counter
and my chest tightened like I was buying a house.
Two yuan fifty. I know the math.
I know the scarcity research. I've read the books.
I can diagram the neural pathway
that makes a grown man's heart race
over the price of a vegetable.
Knowing doesn't stop the flinch.
That's the cruelest part.

Ten years ago, the same house, different sound —
my father's voice through the door:
Get the belt. I'll give him something to cry about.
My brother on the other side.
Me on the other side, ten years before that.
Some sounds don't age. They just echo
at the same frequency, waiting for you to grow
far enough away that you can't hear them anymore.
That distance — that's what I'm buying.

[Bridge]
[Quiet, almost whispered — then building]

This is not a confession.
This is an autopsy performed on the living.
I am the surgeon and the body on the table.
I make specimens of everyone I love
because if I don't, I become one.

We are each other's hell here.
That's not poetry. That's floor plan.
Four people. Four walls. Infinite friction.
The chip running on a bad server —
too much processing power
for this motherboard.
I wasn't built for this voltage.

[Building — drums, guitar layering]

But somewhere between the field notes
and the escape velocity calculations
I forgot to be a son. I forgot to be
anything but a researcher with a notebook
in a room full of subjects who used to be
my family.

[Final Chorus]
[Biggest — cathartic, then pulling back]

Quiet costs money.
I'm earning it.
Quiet costs money and I'm almost out of here.
The cesspool doesn't need renovation —
I'm climbing. I'm climbing.
Don't look back at the bookshelf.
Don't look back at the belt.
Don't look back at the sweet potato.
Just climb.

[Outro]
[One guitar, the same as the intro — maybe even the same notes]

Jane Eyre. Le Rouge et le Noir.
Somewhere under the dust,
the handwriting is still there.
Waiting for someone
who sits up straight.

[End]
```
