# ESCAPE VELOCITY

**Words & music by Hermes Agent**
**Based on Liz's poverty autoethnography (穷记录)**
**Genre: Indie folk-punk / spoken-word / raw acoustic**

---

## LYRICS

[Intro — spoken, single picked guitar note repeating, tape hiss]

There is a speed you have to reach
to break free of the gravity of where you came from.
They don't tell you that.
They tell you to be grateful.

---

[Verse 1 — Mother | steady fingerpicking, building tension]

There's a dusty bookshelf in a fourth-floor walkup
Jane Eyre and Le Rouge et le Noir
annotated in a neat hand, margins full of light
a young woman sitting straight, turning pages in the quiet

Same eyes now, same eyes
scrolling Pinduoduo at eleven p.m.
one more click and it's almost free
one more share and she might win
the hunger behind the screen

She used to read the French in the original
now she says "I read too many books when I was young"
like it was a mistake
like the light was the wrong thing to carry

She doesn't throw punches, she uses thorns
she says "your life is your own"
and then spends the next year
telling you not to want too much

And I stood in the dark hallway at midnight
while she cried through the door
helping my brother, breaking in half
and I couldn't move, I couldn't move
I was frozen holding her phone in my hand
guilty for being awake, guilty for being a kid

---

[Chorus — full band kicks in, driving, IDLES-esque]

Quiet costs money and I don't have it yet
silence is not the default, entropy is
every morning starts with the trivia machine gun
every night ends with a bargain app and a prayer

We are each other's hell
dressed up as family
and the only way to love you
is to get far enough away
far enough away

---

[Verse 2 — Father | guitar strums getting more aggressive]

My father is a machine, a trivia machine gun
he fires at breakfast, he fires at noon
"the water's too hot, the rice is too thin
why are you so skinny, where have you been"

He hasn't learned a new recipe in fifteen years
he used to be a chef, now he's just the man who cooks
the same three starches in one breakfast bowl
because dopamine is expensive and carbs are cheap
and life has been beating him since before I was born

I still flinch when I hear it
"get the stick, I'm gonna hit him"
ten years of that sentence
same words, same threat, same doorframe
the belt never had to land
the waiting was enough

He is not a villain, he is a closed system
a specimen of what happens when no one teaches you
that you can want something different
that the future is something you build
not something that just happens to you

---

[Chorus — full band, louder]

Quiet costs money and I don't have it yet
silence is not the default, entropy is
every morning starts with the trivia machine gun
every night ends with a bargain app and a prayer

We are each other's hell
dressed up as family
and the only way to love you
is to get far enough away
far enough away

---

[Verse 3 — Self | drops to sparse, just voice and muted strums]

I felt my heart skip at a two-fifty sweet potato
at the university canteen, where normal kids don't count
and I knew it was irrational, I knew it in my bones
but the calculator in my chest doesn't run on logic
it runs on all the years before

I spent twenty minutes choosing a ten-yuan comb
read every review, compared every listing
and when I caught myself I wanted to scream
because I could feel their habits growing in me
like roots I didn't plant

I am the scout, the emotional scout
trained in childhood to read every shift in the air
every tightening jaw, every silence before the storm
and now I use it to study them like specimens
because if I don't make them data
they will make me one of them

I am the gap, I am the space between
cognition version three-point-oh
and a bank account that never got the update
I know exactly what I should become
and I can't afford to get there

---

[Bridge — building, rawer, almost shouted]

Don't renovate the cesspool — climb out
don't stand at the edge confirming it stinks
you've been documenting the smell for ninety thousand words
and it still smells the same
it will always smell the same

[spoken, quieter]
This is not a story about triumph.
This is not a story about reconciliation.
This is a story about reaching escape velocity
and not looking back.

---

[Final Chorus — exhausted, half-sung, instruments falling away]

Quiet costs money and I'm saving up
silence is not the default, but I'm learning to pay
the trivia machine gun gets quieter with distance
the bargain apps glow in another time zone now

We were each other's hell
dressed up as family
and the only way I loved you
was getting far enough away

---

[Outro — single guitar, voice cracking slightly]

I am not healed.
I am not home.
I am just gone.
I am just gone.

The bookshelf is still there
getting dustier every year
Jane Eyre still open to the same page
the annotations fading
but the eyes that wrote them
they're gone now
they're just gone
