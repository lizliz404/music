1) WHAT / WHY / HOW

WHAT:
Both v1s are doing the same core job: convert a 90-yuan overnight into a portable thesis — wealth is not marble, it is editorial control over the visible world — then pivot scarcity-shock into ignition ("why not me?"). A is the long documentary cut; B is the compressed claim cut.

WHY it works:
- The concept is load-bearing and non-obvious. "Edit rights" upgrades envy into a mechanism people can feel in their body (soft-close doors, B1 trash, empty streets).
- Source fidelity is high: auntie / Toronto kid / KFC-next-to-gold / fake vines / double "no entry" are specific enough to smell like lived notes, not thinkpiece wallpaper.
- Emotional destination is correct: not resentment anthem, not moral lecture — ignition.

WHY it doesn't fully land yet:
- A explains the thesis in the chorus ("It's not the marble, it's not the lake"). Literarily clean; melodically a little essayistic. The ear wants a shorter claim you can hum without understanding the sociology.
- B's hook is action-strong ("cut / fold / delete") but the verses still do inventory dump in miniature — auntie stanza then tutor stanza, same list energy as A with less air to feel overwhelm.
- Both underuse the sleep/scarcity counter-angle from raw-sleep-experiment: the person who cannot edit noise, mattress, roommates — "不够强，没得选." That is the B-side of edit rights and would widen resonance without adding jargon.
- Bridge problem: A drifts toward second inventory (vine + gardener + night market). B hits the scarcity/abundance collision cleanly but stays thin — one good couplet, then the question. Needs one core + one question, with body, not vocabulary.

HOW effects are achieved (when they hit):
- Concrete sensory entry (magnetic close, B1 heat, green plates) → cognitive label ("edit rights") → social vignettes → scarcity/abundance contrast → reframed "why not me."
- Best single mechanisms: A's "glitch in the code" (outsider-in-frame); B's "Abundance walks soft — scarcity keeps scorecards" (the only line that names the psychology without preaching).
- Style prompts already understand dynamic arc better than most Suno briefs; A's smolder and B's hard-cut are both viable production strategies.

2) 分类 (genre / mechanism class)

Class: observational ignition pop — specifically "field-note → thesis → claim."
Not protest rap, not ballad-of-poverty, not luxury cosplay. Closest mechanism neighbors: late-night alt-pop / art-pop that smuggles class cognition inside a singable hook; documentary verse + chantable chorus.
Risk class if mishandled: LinkedIn-sociology-set-to-music (曲高和寡) OR generic aspiration chorus with tourist imagery (清汤寡水 / Sheeran-mean).

3) Iterate vs stop — verdict

继续磨 — once.
Marginal-return inflection: after ONE v2 that (a) shortens the hook into something hummable under anxiety, (b) kills verse inventory in favor of overwhelm + one vignette that burns, (c) gives bridge a single emotional core + single central question, (d) lets the final section transform the opening image — STOP.
Further polishing beyond that enters perfectionism: swapping synonyms for "friction," adding more Goffman, lengthening for literary completeness. Art is subjective; the market signal you want is whether a stranger can hum "why not me / edit rights" on a commute and feel quieter, not whether every source detail got a cameo.

4) Market resonance

Yes — if the hook stays human and the sociology stays underneath.
Widest resonance levers already present: housing anxiety, "borrowed night in a richer life," quiet envy flipping to agency. Universal enough for anyone who has stood in a nicer lobby and felt small.
Failure modes that shrink audience: over-indexing on compound jargon (entropy / frontstage / 拟剧论), listing rich-kid props without feeling, ending in smug manifesto. Success mode: body first, thesis second, ignition last — useful (安定/释怀 under digital class anxiety) and interesting (hook + one unexpected image that sticks).

SUNO STYLE PROMPT

Style:
Dark mid-tempo alt-pop with a late-night hip-hop pocket, 94 BPM, F minor. Vocal persona: a dry, close-mic observer who starts cataloging like evidence, floods into overwhelm, then hardens into a quiet grin of hunger — not rage, not sermon. Verses: muted electric guitar ticks, sterile pad, tight kick, almost-spoken low alto intimacy, zero theatrical vibrato. Pre-chorus: filtered hats enter, sub-bass warms, breath tightens. Chorus: thick bass, gated clap, stacked doubles on "Edit rights," melody short and chantable in mid-chest — claim you can hum on a commute. Bridge: strip to bare vocal + one piano note + distant night-market hum under the floor. Breakdown: whisper + tongue-click + kick return. Final chorus: full low-end surge, harmony stack on last "Why not me?," then hard cut on the door-slam — no fade. Arc: hush → flood → bare → claim → cut. Dry, mid-forward, addictive hook over literary detail.

Exclude:
Warm acoustic folk, bright major-key pop, Sheeran-style acoustic strum anthems, trap hi-hat spam, EDM drops, gospel belting, overly sweet R&B runs, lo-fi chillhop wallpaper, cinematic string swells, stadium rock bombast, chipmunk pitched vocals, fade-out endings, male radio-baritone crooner, lecture-spoken word without melody
