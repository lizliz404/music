VERSION A — SUNO STYLE PROMPT (Lyric)
═══════════════════════════════════════

Style:
Alternative R&B with lo-fi hip-hop undertones, 92 BPM, D minor. Opens with sparse muted guitar and room-tone ambience — the sound of an empty marble lobby. Male vocals, low register, conversational and slightly detached in verses, like someone narrating a documentary to themselves. Pre-chorus adds subtle sub-bass pulse and filtered hi-hats, tension building. Chorus opens into warm analog synth pads with a hummable melodic hook — the vocal lifts but stays controlled, not belting, more like a quiet realization than an anthem. Second verse introduces a soft boom-bap drum pattern. Bridge strips to near-silence: just finger snaps on 2&4 and a single sustained synth drone, vocals drop to almost-whisper, then the final chorus returns fuller with layered harmonies and a crisp tongue-click fill on the turnaround. Outro fades with vinyl crackle and a single piano note. Overall mood: quiet observation turning into quiet fire. Not aggressive — smoldering.

Exclude:
EDM drops, trap hi-hat rolls, auto-tune, screamo, belting, power ballad, country twang, orchestral swell, dubstep

VERSION B — SUNO STYLE PROMPT (Grok 4.5, self-written)
═══════════════════════════════════════

Style:
Dark indie-electronic art-pop, 98 BPM, A minor. Female vocal persona: dry-eyed observer who catalogs details like evidence, then cracks into clinical hunger — not theatrical rage. Verses: tight 808, clipped muted guitar, sterile pads, half-whispered close-mic, almost spoken, zero vibrato. Chorus: bass thickens, gated clap, stacked doubles on "Edit rights," melody lifts but stays locked — claim, not plea. Bridge: bare vocal + single piano + distant night-market hum. Final chorus: full surge, distorted low-end, shout-harmony on last "Why not me?," then hard cut — no fade, door slam. Dry, punchy, mid-forward; arc: whisper → claim → bare → roar → cut.

Exclude:
Warm acoustic folk, bright major-key pop, trap hi-hat spam, EDM drops, gospel runs, overly sweet R&B belting, lo-fi chillhop, cinematic strings swells, male vocal, fade-out endings, stadium rock bombast, chipmunk pitched vocals
