## Lyrics

```
[Intro]
(single fingerpicked guitar, one figure, tape hiss)
Fourth floor, Hengyang, a shelf against the wall,
an inch of dust and two soft spines —
Jane Eyre, and the red-and-black one
nobody in this house can pronounce anymore.

[Verse 1 — Mother]
There's handwriting in the margins, small and sure,
a whole dictionary pencilled into the white —
every strange word underlined, defined, made hers,
a girl sitting up straight, a book held to the light.
I never saw her like that. I built her back from dust.

Same eyes — I'd know them anywhere.
Now she's up past midnight hunting a two-yuan win,
one more share, one more slice off the price,
"help me cut it down — just one more click."
She says she read too many books when she was young,
as if the dust could teach her how to run,
like the light was something
she was never meant to carry.

[Pre-Chorus]
So I write it down like it happened to somebody else.
Call it fieldwork. Call it keeping clean.

[Chorus]
Quiet costs money, and I don't have it yet.
Loud comes free — the world runs loud by default.
Silence is the thing you buy,
a decibel a day, and I'm behind on all of it.
Quiet costs money; I'm saving up the fare.
The cage door's hung open a while —
I just can't afford to leave.

[Verse 2 — Father]
He opens fire at breakfast — never aims, never has to.
The water's too hot; you drink too much before you eat;
why'd you go and make yourself so thin;
the rice is watery, the hallway's loud, the weather's wrong.
He's been firing since before I had a name,
and the rounds are made of nothing, so they never run out.

Ten years, three starches crowding one black pan —
grain gruel, a fried flat cake, white noodles boiled soft.
Thirty degrees outside and he still boils it down to soup,
because soup is the last recipe left in his hands.
Cheap carbs, quick sugar — the only sweet the day allows.

Then his voice through the door at my brother — the old routine,
ten winters of the same low threat, the same charge building in the air.
I'm grown now, a country away, and I still go still.
The blow was never the part that taught me.
The waiting did the job.

[Pre-Chorus]
So I write it down like it happened to somebody else.
Call it fieldwork. Call it keeping clean.

[Chorus]
Quiet costs money, and I don't have it yet.
Loud comes free — the world runs loud by default.
Silence is the thing you buy,
a decibel a day, and I'm behind on all of it.
Quiet costs money; I'm saving up the fare.
The cage door's hung open a while —
I just can't afford to leave.

[Verse 3 — Self]
Two-fifty for a sweet potato, canteen line, Hangzhou,
and my heart went off like a tripped alarm in a room full of calm.
Nobody else was counting. I was counting.
I've read the whole book on scarcity — I could draw you the wiring,
name the nerve that jumps a grown man's pulse over the price of a vegetable.
Knowing never stops the flinch. That's the joke they leave out of getting smart.

Twenty minutes on a ten-yuan comb. Every review. Every listing.
I felt the habit come up in me like a weed I never planted.
I take the ones I love and turn them into data,
lay them on the page so I stay one page away —
because if I don't make them the study,
I'm scared I turn into the sample.

Cognition three-point-oh; the bank account never got the patch.
I know exactly who I'm supposed to be.
I just can't afford the upgrade yet.

[Bridge]
(voice and guitar alone — no band)
Still a kid, I took my mother's phone in the dark.
Told myself it was only a song to fall asleep to.
That was the lie. It was never the song.
Something I won't put in words — something I'd have died before she saw.
Then a sound in the flat, and every nerve I own pulled tight,
and I froze by the wardrobe with the stolen light in my hand.

And through the bathroom door I heard her come apart —
two in the morning, cleaning my brother up, and crying while she did it,
her voice cracking down on him, folding soft, then cracking down again.
I didn't move. I couldn't. I stood there in the hall
holding a phone that wasn't mine,
guilty for being awake,
guilty for what I'd been doing,
guilty for every year they fed me and carried me
while I hid in the dark and took.

[Final Chorus]
(band returns — driving, not shouting)
Quiet costs money, and I'm still short.
Loud comes free; I'm still living on the free plan.
I've stood at the lip of the pit too long,
naming the smell, taking notes on the stink —
and the pit doesn't want remodeling. It wants me gone.
Leaving's the toll, and I'm still counting out the fare.
I can see the door from here.
Not tonight. Not yet.

[Outro]
(single guitar, mirroring the intro)
Fourth floor, Hengyang. The shelf against the wall.
Jane Eyre. The red-and-black. Another coat of dust.
The handwriting's still there — small, and neat, and sure of something
I keep swearing I'll grow back into.
That shelf will outlast every voice in this house.
Tonight it's outlasting me.
I'm still in the room.
Still counting.

[End]
```

## Suno Style Prompt

```
indie folk with a post-punk drive, spoken-word-adjacent confessional songwriting;
male vocal — weathered, plainspoken and conversational in the verses, pressed and
sung with restraint in the choruses; intensity comes from arrangement density and
rhythmic insistence, never from vocal force; close-mic intimacy, dry room, faint
tape hiss; single fingerpicked acoustic guitar carries intro and verses; brushed
live drums and a walking bass enter at the pre-chorus; a clean, slightly overdriven
electric and steady bass push the chorus forward; the bridge strips back to voice
and one guitar, no band; full band returns for the final chorus as a held breath
released, not an explosion; the outro returns to the lone intro guitar; tempo drifts
up roughly 78–108 BPM across sections; E minor throughout, melancholic but alive,
no triumphant lift; reference points: The Mountain Goats (narrative specificity),
early Bright Eyes (diary-grain intimacy)
```

**Exclude**: `screaming, shouting, shout-catharsis, IDLES-style delivery, autotune, synth pads, backing choir, anthem-rock gloss, sentimental strings, EDM, major-key pop chorus, reverb-drenched vocals, lo-fi mush, garage rock, pop-punk`

## Independent decisions (and how the audit + fact-check were handled)

- **Title kept: "QUIET COSTS."** It reads two ways at once — *quiet costs [you] money* (verb) and *the quiet costs* (noun, the tally of what silence takes). Both are the thesis (安静是需要付费的), so the album title earns its place without a subtitle.
- **Father = images only.** No "specimen / defect / closed system / villain." The verdicts are replaced with source-true scenes: the breakfast nags verbatim in spirit (hot water, drinking before eating, "why so thin," watery rice), the *three starches in one black pan* (grain gruel + fried flat cake + boiled white noodles), and a fresh image not used in v1–v3 — **"thirty degrees outside and he still boils it down to soup, because soup is the last recipe left in his hands"** (from the 6.28 "厨师？不，另一个做饭人罢了 / 开汤" note). It shows cognitive fossilization without ever labeling it.
- **No unverified prop.** The belt/stick is gone. Kept the *verified* core: the 10-year door threat to the brother, the still-present flinch, and the real insight — **"The blow was never the part that taught me. / The waiting did the job."** "The same charge building in the air" carries the 蓄力 / being-watched (战逃反应) that the source actually documents; no weapon is named.
- **"Chef" claim not used.** Handled via the soup/"last recipe left in his hands" image, per the source's rhetorical contrast rather than any biography.
- **Bridge = the unsanitized hallway.** This is the gravity well and it stays dirty: the *stolen* phone, the lie about the song, the furtive thing left unnamed exactly as the source leaves it ("至于是什么事情，咱就不用说了吧"), the freeze by the wardrobe, the mother crying through the bathroom door while cleaning the brother at 2am, and the triple guilt — awake / the thing he was doing / owing them their care (对不起他们的养育). No "not enough of a kid" softening; no "villain loses" gloss (cut per audit).
- **Cesspool restored.** Final chorus: **"I've stood at the lip of the pit too long, naming the smell, taking notes on the stink — and the pit doesn't want remodeling. It wants me gone."** (堵在厕所门口反复确认多臭 + 不要在粪坑里做装修，爬出去).
- **"Leaving's the toll," not "Love was the toll."** Keeps the title thesis intact (the toll is paid in money/quiet, and it's for leaving) instead of overturning it.
- **One sanctioned steal.** "as if the dust could teach her how to run" (best unused mother line, from v2-cursor) is carried in.
- **Ending respects album architecture — stage one.** The narrator sees the door and has *not* walked through: "Leaving's the toll, and I'm still counting out the fare. / I can see the door from here. / Not tonight. Not yet." The outro leaves him **in the room** with the shelf: "Tonight it's outlasting me. / I'm still in the room. / Still counting." No exit, no return, no "almost out."
- **Suno prompt aligns with no-scream.** Post-punk *drive* from arrangement and rhythm, "pressed and sung with restraint," and screaming/shouting/IDLES explicitly excluded.
