## v2 Synthesis Notes

### What was overthrown from v1/v2-cursor/v3

**Father section — verdicts replaced with images**:
All three independent versions used concept-judgment language for the father: "specimen," "defect," "closed system," "villain," "he's not the villain — he's the system's door." These are the weakest lines across all versions — they state a conclusion instead of building a scene. The fix applies the same technique that made "the belt never had to land / the waiting was enough" the strongest line: give the audience a concrete image and let them reach the verdict themselves. "Fifteen years, the same three starches frying in the same black pan" does more work than any thesis statement.

**Bridge — the unsanitized memory**:
v3's hallway/bathroom-door scene was the only raw, unresolved moment across all three versions — the Orion analysis completely sidestepped it. v2 makes it the bridge's emotional core. A song about poverty and family shouldn't avoid the least dignified moment; it should aim directly at it.

**Ending — not "I am just gone," but "strong enough to come back"**:
"I am just gone" is technically clean but emotionally too easy — a cold victory pose. The bookshelf ending ("I left to become someone who could come back and not be swallowed") is harder and more honest. The real goal was never permanent exile; it was getting strong enough to choose.

**Pending fact-check**: The line about the father "used to be a chef" — if this originated from the source's rhetorical contrast ("厨师？不，另一个做饭人罢了" = "Chef? No, just another person who cooks") rather than literal biography, it may not be factually accurate. Liz to verify before final.

## Production Notes

- **Intro/Outro bookend**: Same guitar figure. The shelf is still there — the difference between intro and outro is the narrator, not the room.
- **Verse 2 (Father)**: Gunfire metaphor should feel rhythmic, not angry — the point is the constancy, not the volume. Think driving, not blasting.
- **Bridge**: Should feel genuinely uncomfortable — not performed vulnerability. The hallways/bathroom door memory is the song's emotional center of gravity. Everything before it builds toward it; everything after it is earned by it.
- **Outro modulation to G major**: A small lift — not triumphant, just less heavy. The shelf outlasts everything.
